# 弗莱明 引语集

## 关于青霉素发现

1. "One sometimes finds what one is not looking for. When I woke up just after dawn on September 28, 1928, I certainly didn't plan to revolutionize all medicine by discovering the world's first antibiotic, or bacteria killer. But I suppose that was exactly what I did."
— Alexander Fleming

2. "That's funny."
— Fleming, upon noticing the contaminated culture plate (reported by Merlin Pryce)

3. "Penicillin was not the first antibiotic I happened to discover."
— Alexander Fleming, Nobel Lecture, 11 December 1945

4. "I found it by accident."
— Fleming on penicillin discovery

5. "It is not the doctor who discovers, but the doctor who keeps his eyes open."
— paraphrased sentiment attributed to Fleming regarding his discovery method

## 关于青霉素潜力与抗生素耐药性警告

6. "The time may come when penicillin can be bought by anyone in the shops. Then there is the danger that the ignorant man may easily underdose himself and by exposing his microbes to non-lethal quantities of the drug make them resistant."
— Fleming, Nobel Lecture, 1945

7. "And if you use penicillin, use enough. ...the thoughtless person playing with penicillin is morally responsible for the death of the man who finally succumbs to infection with the penicillin-resistant organism. I hope this evil can be averted."
— Fleming, Nobel Lecture, 1945

8. He cautioned not to use penicillin unless there was a properly diagnosed reason for it to be used, and that if it were used, never to use too little, or for too short a period, since these are the circumstances under which bacterial resistance to antibiotics develops.

## 关于溶菌酶

9. "I choose lysozyme as the subject for this address for two reasons, firstly because I have a fatherly interest in the name, and, secondly, because its importance in connection with natural immunity does not seem to be generally appreciated."
— Fleming, Presidential address at the Royal Society of Medicine, 18 October 1932

10. "In this communication I wish to draw attention to a substance present in the tissues and secretions of the body, which is capable of rapidly dissolving certain bacteria. As this substance has properties akin to those of ferments I have called it a 'Lysozyme'."
— Fleming, "On a remarkable bacteriolytic element found in tissues and secretions", Proceedings of the Royal Society B: Biological Sciences, 1 May 1922

## 关于科学研究风格

11. "[If] he had been as tidy as he thought I was, he would not have made his two great discoveries."
— V. D. Allison, on Fleming's habit of leaving culture plates unattended

12. "The Fleming Myth"
— Fleming's own term for his celebrity status, acknowledging the contributions of Florey, Chain, and Heatley

## 关于消毒剂与伤口治疗

13. "Antiseptics worked well on the surface, but deep wounds tended to shelter anaerobic bacteria from the antiseptic agent, and antiseptics seemed to remove beneficial agents produced that actually protected the patients in these cases at least as well as they removed bacteria, and did nothing to remove the bacteria that were out of reach."
— Fleming, The Lancet, 1917

14. "I have been trying to point out to the medical profession the importance of looking upon wound infection from the point of view of the natural defenses of the body."
— Fleming, on his WWI antiseptic research findings

## 关于团队科学

15. "Without Fleming, no Chain; without Chain, no Florey; without Florey, no Heatley; without Heatley, no penicillin."
— Sir Henry Harris, 1998

## 关于青霉素发现的重要性

16. "The single greatest victory ever achieved over disease."
— Common description of Fleming's penicillin discovery

## 关于天赋与运气

17. "One sometimes finds what one is not looking for."
— Fleming's most famous quote on serendipity in scientific discovery

## 关于丘吉尔的假故事

18. "A wondrous fable."
— Fleming, in a letter to friend and colleague Andre Gratia, denying the popular story that he saved Winston Churchill's life

## 关于弗莱明作为教师

19. "[Fleming was] initially a shy uncommunicative man and a poor lecturer, he blossomed under the attention he received, becoming one of the world's best-known scientists."
— Britannica

## 关于青霉素治疗的临床试验（1930年）

20. Cecil George Paine, a pathologist at the Royal Infirmary in Sheffield and former student of Fleming, was the first to use penicillin successfully for medical treatment. He cured eye infections (conjunctivitis) of one adult and three infants (neonatal conjunctivitis) on 25 November 1930.
