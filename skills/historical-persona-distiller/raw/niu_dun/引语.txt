# 牛顿引语原文（从各传记来源提取）
## 关于自己
1. "I do not know what I may appear to the world; but to myself I seem to have been only like a boy playing on the seashore, and diverting myself now and then in finding a smoother pebble or prettier shell than ordinary, while the great ocean of truth lay all undiscovered before me."
   —— Biography.com, 引自牛顿晚年自述

2. "Plato is my friend, Aristotle is my friend, but my best friend is truth."
   —— Newton, Quaestiones Quaedam Philosophicae (1664), 见于Britannica和MacTutor

3. "If I have seen further it is by standing on the shoulders of giants."
   —— 牛顿名言，广泛记载于Biography.com, History.com等来源

4. "It is the perfection of God's works that they are all done with the greatest simplicity."
   —— 牛顿语录，Biography.com

5. "Every body continues in its state of rest, or of uniform motion in a right line, unless it is compelled to change that state by forces impressed upon it."
   —— Newton's First Law of Motion, Principia 1687

6. "To every action there is always opposed an equal reaction: or, the mutual actions of two bodies upon one another are always equal, and directed to contrary parts."
   —— Newton's Third Law of Motion, Principia 1687

7. "I see I have made myself a slave to philosophy."
   —— 牛顿语录，Biography.com

8. "Truth is ever to be found in simplicity, and not in the multiplicity and confusion of things."
   —— 牛顿语录，Biography.com

9. "Atheism is so senseless and odious to mankind that it never had many professors."
   —— 牛顿语录，Biography.com

10. "The changing of bodies into light, and light into bodies, is very conformable to the course of nature, which seems delighted with transmutations."
    —— 牛顿语录，Biography.com

## 关于方法与科学
11. "Amicus Plato amicus Aristoteles magis amica veritas" ("Plato is my friend, Aristotle is my friend, but my best friend is truth")
    —— 牛顿笔记本 Quaestiones Quaedam Philosophicae，约1664年，MacTutor

12. "the prime of my age for invention" (指1665-1666年瘟疫期间)
    —— 牛顿自述，Newton Institute (Encarta)

## 关于引力
13. "... all matter attracts all other matter with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them."
    —— Principia Mathematica, 1687，MacTutor

14. "Gravity explains the motions of the planets, but it cannot explain who sets the planets in motion."
    —— 牛顿语录，History.com

15. "No great discovery was ever made without a bold guess."
    —— 牛顿语录，History.com

## 致友人/通信中
16. Barrow致Collins的信中描述牛顿："Mr Newton ... very young ... but of an extraordinary genius and proficiency in these things."
    —— MacTutor History of Mathematics, 1669年8月

17. Hooke 1679年信中提出："... that the Attraction always is in a duplicate proportion to the Distance from the Center Reciprocall ..."
    —— MacTutor History of Mathematics

18. "Be courageous and steady to the Laws and you cannot fail."
    —— 牛顿致Vice-Chancellor信（关于抵抗James II国王），Britannica

## 牛顿助手/同代人的描述
19. Whiston描述牛顿："Newton was of the most fearful, cautious and suspicious temper that I ever knew."
    —— MacTutor History of Mathematics

20. Keyenes评价牛顿："Newton was not the first of the age of reason. He was the last of the magicians, the last of the Babylonians and Sumerians, the last great mind that looked out on the visible and intellectual world with the same eyes as those who began to build our intellectual inheritance rather less than 10,000 years ago."
    —— Biography.com，引自John Maynard Keynes

## 牛顿少年时自述（罪过清单，19岁时所写）
21. "Threatening my father and mother Smith to burne them and the house over them."
    —— MacTutor History of Mathematics，引自牛顿1662年罪过清单

22. "... setting my heart on money, learning, and pleasure more than Thee ..."
    —— MacTutor History of Mathematics，引自牛顿罪过清单
