# David Hume 后世评述语料
# 来源：World History Encyclopedia + Stanford Encyclopedia

## 1. 思想影响

"Hume's work influenced many other philosophers, notably Immanuel Kant (1724-1804), who said that after reading Hume's work, he was roused from a 'dogmatic slumber,'"
— World History Encyclopedia

"and the utilitarian Jeremy Bentham (1748-1832), who 'felt as if scales had fallen from my eyes' after reading Hume's treatment of morality."
— World History Encyclopedia

"The unsettling ideas of Hume, which often challenge our basic preconceptions even before we start thinking about complex philosophical problems, along with his appeal to use our senses and study humanity in relation to what we see in nature, have all had increasing appeal over the last two centuries."
— World History Encyclopedia

"the historian A. Gottlieb puts it, Hume intended to 'encourage intellectual modesty'"
— World History Encyclopedia

"Hume presents a positive view of human nature, and he seeks to explain this nature so that more progress may be made in acquiring new knowledge."
— World History Encyclopedia

"Hume believed that humans may possess self-interest but they also naturally possess feelings of sympathy and humanity."
— World History Encyclopedia

"These sentiments and others give humans a natural 'moral sense'."
— World History Encyclopedia

## 2. 社会契约论批判的历史影响

"Hume seems to have favoured a constitutional monarchy as the least worst system of government."
— World History Encyclopedia

## 5. 关于 Hume 的怀疑论

"Almost all the governments which exist at present, or of which there remains any record in story, have been founded originally, either on usurpation or conquest, or both, without any pretence of a fair consent or voluntary subjection of the people."
— World History Encyclopedia

"Hume did not agree with Hobbes, Locke, and others in describing how people got together to form communities using some sort of social contract"
— World History Encyclopedia

## 6. Stanford Encyclopedia 总结

"Hume concludes that no theory of reality is possible; there can be no knowledge of anything beyond experience."
— Stanford Encyclopedia (section summary)

"His Enquiry concerning the Principles of Morals is regarded as his best work"
— Britannica

## 7. 关于休谟的 bundle theory

"Hume does not say that the idea of the soul or the self is false, only that the concept is an empty one because we cannot be precise about it. Hume goes further and suggests that when we think about the soul, that thinking process could very well be the soul (i.e. it does not exist as a thing beyond our flow of thoughts). This is known as the bundle theory"
— World History Encyclopedia
