# Francis Bacon Quotes from Britannica

"Bacon was born January 22, 1561, at York House off the Strand, London, the younger of the two sons of the lord keeper, Sir Nicholas Bacon, by his second marriage."

"He is remembered in literary terms for the sharp worldly wisdom of a few dozen essays; by students of constitutional history for his power as a speaker in Parliament and in famous trials and as James I's lord chancellor; and intellectually as a man who claimed all knowledge as his province."

"Bacon has been reprobated for having taken part in the examination under torture of Peacham, which turned out to be fruitless."

"Bacon commented to Buckingham: 'I acknowledge the sentence just, and for reformation's sake fit, the justest Chancellor that hath been in the five changes since Sir Nicolas Bacon's time.' The magnanimity and wit of the epigram sets his case against the prevailing standards."

"In adversity showed patience, unimpaired intellectual vigour, and fortitude."

"Finally, in March 1626, driving one day near Highgate (a district to the north of London) and deciding on impulse to discover whether snow would delay the process of putrefaction, he stopped his carriage, purchased a hen, and stuffed it with snow. He was seized with a sudden chill, which brought on bronchitis, and he died at the earl of Arundel's house nearby on April 9, 1626."

"Physical deprivation distressed him but what hurt most was the loss of favour."

"Bacon has been described as the father of empirical philosophy."

"He claimed that knowledge should be 'referred to action' and that the true end of knowledge was 'the reliefe of Mans estate.'"

"He is best known for his essays, which offer a pragmatic and realistic perspective on human behavior and governance, as well as for his significant contributions to the philosophy of science."

"However, his political ambitions ended abruptly in 1621 when he was accused of corruption, leading to his fall from grace. He was convicted of bribery, fined, and briefly imprisoned in the Tower of London, although King James, I later pardoned him."

"He set out principles of induction that became fundamental to the scientific method."

"The Four Idols (Idols of the Tribe, Cave, Marketplace, and Theatre) represent the preconceptions that distort human reasoning."

"From his letter to Lord Burghley in 1591: 'I have as vast contemplative ends, as I have moderate civil ends' and 'I have taken all knowledge to be my province.'"

"From Novum Organum: 'human knowledge and human power come to the same thing' and 'truth and utility are … the very things themselves.'"

"From Instauratio Magna: 'the only course left was to try to do everything again with better assistance, and undertake a wholesale Instauration of the sciences, arts and all human learning, raised on proper foundations.'"

"From The Advancement of Learning: 'the Gouernements of Princes in minority … haue neuertheless excelled the gouernement of Princes of mature age' and 'the Soueraignetie of man lyeth hidd in knowledge, wherein many things are reserued which kings with their treasures cannot buy nor with theire forces Commande.'"

"From The Wisdom of the Ancients (1609): about hidden practical meaning embodied in ancient myths."

"From his Commentarius Solutus ('Loose Commentary'): 'like a Marchant's wast booke where to enter all maner of remembrance of matter, fourme, business, study.'"

"From his late writings: 'If I be left to myself I will graze and bear natural philosophy.'"
