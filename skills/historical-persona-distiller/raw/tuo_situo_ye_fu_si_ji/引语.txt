# 陀思妥耶夫斯基 — 直接引语原文
# 来源: Britannica, Biography.com, 以及其他学术来源

## 身份与出生背景
"Born Nov. 11, 1821 in Moscow, Russia, Fyodor Dostoyevsky was educated at home until 1833."
— Biography.com

## 关于癫痫发作
"The major events of Dostoevsky's life—mock execution, imprisonment in Siberia, and epileptic seizures—were so well known that, even apart from his work, Dostoyevsky achieved great celebrity in his own time."
— Britannica

## 关于其文学方向
"Dostoyevsky is usually regarded as one of the finest novelists who ever lived."
— Britannica

## 关于心理深度
"Dostoyevsky specialized in the analysis of pathological states of mind that lead to insanity, murder, and suicide and in the exploration of the emotions of humiliation, self-destruction, tyrannical domination, and murderous rage."
— Britannica

## 关于其预言之声
"His works are often called prophetic because he so accurately predicted how Russia's revolutionaries would behave if they came to power."
— Britannica

## 关于地下人/存在主义先驱
"Notes from the Underground (1864) is best known for its ant-Hero protagonist."
— Britannica

## 关于《卡拉马佐夫兄弟》
"The youngest legitimate son, Alyosha, is another of Dostoyevsky's attempts to create a realistic Christ figure."
— Britannica

"In 'Rebellion,' Ivan indicts God the Father for creating a world in which children suffer."
— Britannica

"The image of the 'petty demon' has had immense influence on 20th-century thought and literature."
— Britannica

## 关于基督信仰
"陀思妥耶夫斯基本人曾明确地表示：如果有谁向我证明，基督脱离了真理，并且的确是真理也脱离了基督，那我宁愿与基督而不是与真理在一起。"
— 《转喻的辩证法：陀思妥耶夫的斯基的宗教修辞》，北京大学期刊网

## 关于其苦役经历
"Unlike many other Russian writers of the first part of the 19th century, Dostoyevsky was not born into the landed gentry."
— Britannica

## 关于其写作速度与贫困
"Dostoyevsky was always in need of money and had to hurry his works into publication."
— Britannica

## 关于其影响力
"Literary modernism, existentialism, and various schools of psychology, theology, and literary criticism have been profoundly shaped by his ideas."
— Britannica

## 关于复调小说理论
"在《陀思妥耶夫斯基诗学问题》中，巴赫金谈到复调小说的源头和小说体裁形成的最早阶段——古希腊罗马的庄谐体体裁"
— 中国社会科学网

## 关于宗教情感
"Dostoyevsky's lifelong attachment to religion began with the old-fashioned piety of his family."
— Britannica

## 关于《群魔》中的反乌托邦预言
"Starting with unlimited freedom, I arrive at unlimited despotism." — Shigalyov, in The Possessed

"When the revolution comes, 'Cicero will have his tongue cut out, Copernicus will have his eyes put out, Shakespeare will be stoned.'" — Pyotr Verkhovensky

## 关于阿廖沙·卡拉马佐夫
"The narrator proclaims him the work's real hero, but readers are usually most interested in the middle brother, the intellectual Ivan."
— Britannica

## 关于"一切都被允许"
"if there is no God and no immortality, then 'all is permitted.'" — Ivan Karamazov

## 关于忓悔
"the prisoner Raskolnikov, who has confessed not out of remorse but out of emotional stress, at first continues to maintain his amoral theories but at last is brought to true repentance by a revelatory dream and by Sonya's goodness." — Britannica on Crime and Punishment
