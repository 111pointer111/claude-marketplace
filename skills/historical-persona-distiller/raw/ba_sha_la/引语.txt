# 帕斯卡（Blaise Pascal）引语集

## 来源：Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Pascal

### 关于虚空
"The eternal silence of these infinite spaces frightens me."
（这无限空间的永恒沉默令我恐惧。）

### 关于哲学的价值
"we do not believe the whole of philosophy to be worth one hour's effort"
（我们不相信整个哲学值得一个小时的努力。）

### 关于信仰与理性的关系
"if one submits everything to reason, our religion will contain nothing that is mysterious or supernatural"
（如果把一切都交给理性，我们的宗教将不包含任何神秘或超自然的东西。）

### 关于宗教多样性的观察
"I see several inconsistent religions, all of which except one are false. Each one wishes to be believed on the basis of its own authority and threatens unbelievers. I therefore do not believe them for that reason."
（我看到几个不一致的宗教，除了一个之外都是假的。每一个都希望基于自己的权威被相信，并威胁不信者。因此，我不会仅仅因此相信它们。）

### 关于教会
"outside of which I am fully convinced there is no salvation"
（我完全确信，教会之外没有救恩。）

### 关于形而上学证明
"The metaphysical proofs … have little value"
（形而上学的证明……几乎没有价值。）

### 关于恩典
"By sufficient grace you mean a grace that is not sufficient"
（所谓充足的恩典，你的意思是一种并不充足的恩典。）

### 关于知识与情欲
"the investigation of nature's secrets (which are irrelevant to us), knowledge of which is useless, and which men do not wish to know except for the sake of knowing them"
（对自然界秘密的探究（这些秘密与我们无关），其知识是无用的，人们除了为知识本身而别无他由地不愿知道它们。）

### 关于事实的知识
"matters of fact are proved only by the senses" and "easily decided"
（事实问题只能通过感官来证明，而且容易判断。）

### 关于教皇的权威
"authority is useless in that context"
（在这种情境中权威是无用的。）

### 关于伽利略
"It was in vain therefore that the Jesuits obtained from Rome a decree against Galileo … That is not what will prove that the earth does not move; and if one had unchanging observations that proved the earth revolves, all the men in the world could not stop it moving"
（因此，耶稣会从罗马获得对伽利略的法令是徒劳的……那并不能证明地球不运动；如果有不变的观察证明地球在运动，世界上所有人都无法阻止它运动。）

### 关于自然的秘密
"the secrets of nature are hidden"
（自然的秘密是被隐藏的。）

### 关于科学进步
"make continual progress in science in proportion as the world gets older"
（科学不断进步，与世界的变老成正比。）

### 关于自然的道德法则
"There are undoubtedly natural laws, but our fine corrupted reason has corrupted everything"
（无疑有自然法则，但我们精致腐败的理性已经腐败了一切。）

### 关于给予穷人施舍的义务
"we are obliged by justice to give alms from our surplus, to alleviate even the common necessities of the poor"
（我们出于正义有义务从剩余中给予施舍，以减轻穷人共同的急需。）

### 关于意图决定道德品质
"it is the intention which determines the [moral] quality of an action"
（意图决定了行动的道德品质。）

### 关于概率主义
"'probable' in this context had little to do with calculations of probability, but was defined as 'everything that is approved by well-known authors'"
（在这种语境中，"可能的"与概率计算关系不大，而是被定义为"被知名作者批准的任何事情"。）

### 关于情欲与力量
"concupiscence and force are the sources of all our actions. Concupiscence causes voluntary actions, and force causes those that are involuntary"
（情欲和力量是我们所有行为的来源。情欲导致自愿行为，力量导致非自愿行为。）

### 关于政治保守主义
"the worst evil of all is civil war"
（最糟糕的恶是内战。）

### 关于服从
"The Church … has always taught her children not to render evil for evil … to obey magistrates and superiors, even those who are unjust, because we must always respect in them the power of God who has set them over us"
（教会……始终教导她的孩子不要以恶报恶……要服从地方官和上级，即使那些不公正的，因为我们在他们身上总是要尊敬上帝赐予我们的权力。）

### 关于信仰的恩赐
"the capacity of humans to believe the theological interpretation on which the implied worldview depended"
（人类相信所暗示世界观所依赖的神学解释的能力。）

### 关于信仰超越理性
"faith provides appropriately disposed Christians with a means to transcend the limits of what is intelligible and to accept as true even matters that they cannot understand"
（信仰为适当准备的基督徒提供了一种超越可理解性限制的方法，并接受他们无法理解的事情为真。）

### 关于自由的恩典
"Human beings, by their own nature, always have the power to sin and to resist grace, and since the time of their corruption they always have an unfortunate depth of concupiscence which infinitely increases this power of resistance. Nevertheless, when it pleases God to touch them with his mercy, He makes them do what he wants them to do and in the manner in which he wishes them to act, without this infallibility of God's operation destroying in any way the natural freedom of human beings … That is how God disposes the free will of human beings without imposing any necessity on them, and how free will, which can always resist grace but does not always wish to do so, directs itself both freely and infallibly towards God."
（人按其本性，总是拥有犯罪和抵抗恩典的能力，自从他们堕落以来，他们总是有一种不幸的深度情欲，无限地增加了这种抵抗能力。然而，当上帝以慈悲触摸他们时，他使他们做他要他们做的事，并以他希望他们行动的方式行动，而上帝运作的这种无误性丝毫不摧毁人的自然自由……这就是上帝在不强加任何必然性的情况下安排人的自由意志的方式，以及自由意志——它总是可以抵抗恩典但并非总是愿意这样做——如何自由地同时无误地向上帝指引自己。）

### 关于预定的恩典
God predestines some human beings for salvation and saves them by "means that are certain and infallible"
（上帝预定一些人得救，并通过确定无误的手段拯救他们。）

## 来源：World History Encyclopedia

### 关于古代人
"Those whom we call ancient were really new in all things, and properly constituted the infancy of mankind; and as we have joined to their knowledge the experience of the centuries which have followed them, it is in ourselves that we should find this antiquity that we revere in others."
（我们称之为古代人的实际上在所有事情上都是新的，他们正确地构成了人类的婴儿期；既然我们已经把他们的知识与继他们之后的几个世纪的经验结合在一起，我们应该在自身中找到我们在他人身上尊敬的这种古代性。）

### 帕斯卡赌注原文
"If you gain, you gain all. If you lose, you lose nothing. Wager then, without hesitation, that He exists."
（如果你赢了，你就赢得一切。如果你输了，你什么也不失去。那么，就毫不犹豫地赌他存在吧。）

### 关于想象与理性
帕斯卡认为，上帝不是通过对自然法则的研究被认识的，而是通过对违反这些法则的奇迹的显现被认识的。帕斯卡认为，对于他来说，一个人能够认识上帝的不是通过理性，而是通过个人的想象力或直觉。
