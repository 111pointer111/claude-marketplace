# 哈耶克经典引语集

## 关于市场与价格

1. **价格信号隐喻**: "To think that an authority needs to decide what the price of rice ought to be is like thinking that an authority needs to decide what sound people ought to make when they want to refer to rice." — Hayek (1945)

2. **价格是信号而非命令**: "Prices are signals, not marching orders." — Garrison and Kirzner interpreting Hayek (1987)

3. **市场作为发现程序**: "The market tends to use this tacit knowledge through a type of discovery procedure, by which this information is unknowingly transmitted throughout the economy as an unintended consequence of individuals' pursuing their own ends." — Hayek (1948b)

4. **知识的分散性**: "It is rather a problem of how to secure the best use of resources known to any of the members of society, for ends whose relative importance only those individuals know. Or, to put it briefly, it is a problem of the utilization of knowledge not given to anyone in its totality." — Hayek (1945)

## 关于自发秩序

5. **人类行动而非人类设计**: "It would be no exaggeration to say that social theory begins with—and has an object only because of—the discovery that there exist orderly structures which are the product of the action of many men but are not the result of human design." — Hayek (1973)

6. **亚当·弗格森的引用**: "[Adam Smith's] mentor Adam Ferguson wrote: 'These various institutions ... are the result of human action but not the execution of any human design.'" — Hayek

7. **自发秩序的两类型**: Hayek used the Greek terms cosmos for spontaneous order and taxis for consciously planned organization.

## 关于社会主义与中央计划

8. **读了米塞斯《社会主义》后的体验**: "To none of us young men who read the book when it appeared, the world was ever the same again." — Hayek (1956)

9. **经济计算问题**: "A claim for equality of material position can be met only by a government with totalitarian powers." — Hayek

10. **米塞斯论点的核心**: "Mises's central thesis was not that socialism is impossible, but that it cannot achieve an efficient utilization of resources." — Hayek (1992)

## 关于商业周期

11. **货币与市场不稳定**: "The past instability of the market economy is the consequence of the exclusion of the most important regulator of the market mechanism, money, from itself being regulated by the market process." — Hayek (1931)

12. **人工繁荣的自我逆转**: "Every artificial boom induced by credit expansion, then, is self-reversing." — Hayek

## 关于自由与政府

13. **自由的定义**: Hayek was concerned with "that condition of men in which coercion of some by others is reduced as much as is possible in society" — The Constitution of Liberty (1960)

14. **两种自由主义**: "Mises himself was still much more a child of the rationalist tradition of the Enlightenment and of continental, rather than of English, liberalism ... than I am myself." — Hayek (1978a)

## 关于思想影响

15. **一战对思想的决定性影响**: "The decisive influence was really World War I. It's bound to draw your attention to the problems of political organization." — Hayek

16. **维特根斯坦的影响**: "Hayek said that Wittgenstein's philosophy and methods of analysis had a profound influence on his own life and thought." — Ebenstein (2001)

## 关于凯恩斯与论战

17. **凯恩斯对《价格与生产》的评价**: "One of the most frightful muddles I have ever read... an extraordinary example of how, starting with a mistake, a remorseless logician can end in Bedlam." — Keynes, Economic Journal

## 关于诺贝尔奖

18. **对获奖的惊讶**: "He was surprised at being given the award and believed that he was given it with Myrdal to balance the award with someone from the opposite side of the political spectrum." — Nobel biography

19. **获奖演说的忧虑**: "He spoke with apprehension at his award speech about the danger the authority of the prize would lend to an economist." — Nobel biography

## 关于晚年与影响

20. **撒切尔与《自由宪章》**: "Thatcher 'reached into her briefcase and took out a book. It was Hayek's The Constitution of Liberty. "This", she said sternly, "is what we believe", and banged Hayek down on the table.'" — Ebenstein (2001)

21. **里根对哈耶克的评价**: "President Reagan listed Hayek as among the two or three people who most influenced his philosophy." — Ebenstein

22. **最快乐的的一天**: "I've just had the happiest day of my life." — Hayek, after meeting the Queen (1984)
