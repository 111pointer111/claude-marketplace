# 希波克拉底引语集
# 综合多来源整理

## 直接引语（原文溯源）

1. "I will keep this Oath and this stipulation... I will reckon him who taught me this Art equally dear to me as my parents."
   — Hippocratic Oath, Francis Adams translation (1849), Britannica

2. "I will follow that system of regimen which, according to my ability and judgment, I consider for the benefit of my patients, and abstain from whatever is deleterious and mischievous."
   — Hippocratic Oath, Britannica

3. "I will give no deadly medicine to any one if asked, nor suggest any such counsel."
   — Hippocratic Oath, Britannica

4. "I will not cut persons laboring under the stone, but will leave this to be done by men who are practitioners of this work."
   — Hippocratic Oath, Britannica

5. "Into whatever houses I enter, I will go into them for the benefit of the sick, and will abstain from every voluntary act of mischief and corruption."
   — Hippocratic Oath, Britannica

6. "Whatever, in connection with my professional practice or not, in connection with it, I see or hear, in the life of men, which ought not to be spoken of abroad, I will not divulge, as reckoning that all such should be kept secret."
   — Hippocratic Oath, Britannica

7. "While I continue to keep this Oath unviolated, may it be granted to me to enjoy life and the practice of the art, respected by all men, in all times!"
   — Hippocratic Oath, Britannica

8. "Κάλλιον το προλαμβάνειν του θεραπεύειν" (It is better to prevent than to treat a disease)
   — Hippocratic Corpus, cited in PMC11526839 academic review article

9. "the brain was the organ responsible for intelligence and consciousness"
   — Hippocratic Corpus, cited in PMC11526839

10. "walking was the best available medicine"
    — Hippocratic Corpus, cited in PMC11526839

## 后世评价引语

12. "Hippocrates was the first physician in history to establish medicine as a science."
    — PMC11526839 abstract

13. "Hippocrates still continues to represent the humane, ethical aspects of the medical profession."
    — Britannica, Influence section

14. "Hippocrates was called 'the Great Physician' but that he was small in stature."
    — Aristotle, Politics (as cited in Britannica)

15. Plato called Hippocrates "the Asclepiad of Cos" who taught students for fees.
    — Plato, Protagoras (as cited in Britannica)

16. "Hippocrates was the first to separate medicine from philosophy."
    — Cornelius Celsus (as cited in World History Encyclopedia)

17. "The simple and direct writings of the Hippocratic Collection read well as sample empirical texts that eschewed dogma."
    — Britannica, Influence section

18. "Hippocrates considered that patients consisted of body, mind, and spirit."
    — PMC11526839, Holistic Approach section

19. "The ancient Greeks believed that all maladies started from the gut."
    — PMC11526839, Prevention of Disease section
