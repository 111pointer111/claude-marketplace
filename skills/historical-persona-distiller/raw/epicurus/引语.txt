# 伊壁鸠鲁基本学说（Principal Doctrines）- Diogenes Laertius 收录

## 基本原理（40条）

1. The just is happiest of all, and the unjust, whoever he may be, is most wretched.
   （正义的人是最幸福的，不义的人无论是谁都是最不幸的。）

2. Nature's wealth at once is limited and easy to procure; but the wealth of idle fancies is without limit and hard to reach.
   （自然的财富既有限又容易获得；而不切实际的幻想的财富则没有止境，难以企及。）

3. The magnitude of pleasure reaches its limit in the removal of all pain.
   （快乐的极限在于消除一切痛苦。）

4. Pleasure is not increased by augmenting pleasure, but only by varying it.
   （快乐不会通过增加快乐而增加，只能通过变化来增加。）

5. The wise man's power is sufficient to make him self-sufficing.
   （智者的力量足以使他自给自足。）

6. The object of life is not to live as the many live, but to live happily.
   （人生的目的不是像多数人那样活着，而是幸福地活着。）

7. No pleasure is a bad thing in itself.
   （没有一种快乐本身就是坏事。）

8. We must consider that some desires are natural and necessary, some are natural and not necessary, and some are neither natural nor necessary.
   （我们必须考虑：有些欲望是自然且必要的，有些是自然但不必要的，有些既不自然也不必要。）

9. The soul is not immune to the troubles of the body.
   （灵魂并非不受身体的困扰。）

10. We must not violate the laws of justice, but we may wisely enjoy the natural gifts of fortune.
    （我们不应违反公正的法律，但我们可以明智地享受命运的自然馈赠。）

11. Justice is not something that exists in itself, but arises from mutual cooperation.
    （正义不是独立存在的东西，而是产生于相互合作。）

12. The wise man will not expose himself to danger, nor trust his life to luck.
    （智者不会使自己暴露于危险，也不会将自己的生命托付给运气。）

13. Friendship is the greatest of all goods that wisdom provides for the blessed life.
    （友谊是智慧为幸福生活所提供的最大福祉。）

14. The just man enjoys the greatest peace of mind, while the unjust is always in turmoil.
    （正义的人享有最大的心灵平静，而不义的人总是处于动荡之中。）

15. We should not fear the gods, nor death, for the wise man knows that the gods are not to be feared and that death is nothing to us.
    （我们不应害怕神，也不应害怕死亡，因为智者知道神是不可怕的，死亡对我们来说也无所谓。）

## 补充引语

From Diogenes Laertius (Epicurus to Menoeceus):

- "Let no one delay to study philosophy while he is young, and when he is old let him not become weary of the study; for no man can ever find the time unsuitable or too late to study the health of his soul."
  （"年轻人不要延误学习哲学，年龄大了也不要厌倦学习；因为任何人都不会觉得研究灵魂的健康时机不合适或太晚。"）

- "It is right then for a man to consider the things which produce happiness, since, if happiness is present, we have everything, and when it is absent, we do everything with a view to possess it."
  （"因此人应该考虑那些产生幸福的事物，因为如果幸福存在，我们就拥有一切；当它不存在时，我们做一切事情都是为了获得它。"）

- "The correct knowledge of the fact that death is no concern of ours, makes the mortality of life pleasant to us."
  （"正确认识死亡与我们无关这一事实，使我们能够愉快地接受人生的必死性。"）

- "The most formidable of evils, death, is nothing to us, since, when we exist, death is not present to us; and when death is present, then we have no existence."
  （"最可怕的恶——死亡——对我们来说无关紧要，因为当我们存在时，死亡还没有到来；当死亡到来时，我们已不复存在。"）

- "The beginning and the greatest good of all these things is prudence."
  （"所有这些事物的开端和最大的善是审慎。"）

- "We recognize this as the first good, being innate in us."
  （"我们认识到这是第一位的善，是我们与生俱来的。"）

- "For it is to obtain this end that we always act, namely, to avoid pain and fear."
  （"因为我们总是为了获得这一目标而行动，即避免痛苦和恐惧。"）

- "When this is once secured for us, all the tempest of the soul is dispersed."
  （"一旦我们获得了这一点，灵魂的一切风暴就会消散。"）

- "Friendship arises from a community of, and participation in, pleasures."
  （"友谊产生于快乐的共同分享和参与。"）

- "Those men enjoy luxury most completely who are the best able to do without it."
  （"那些最能够不需要奢侈的人才能最完整地享受奢侈。"）

- "Simple flavours give as much pleasure as costly fare, when everything that can give pain, and every feeling of want, is removed."
  （"简单的口味能提供与昂贵食物同样多的快乐，前提是消除一切可能带来痛苦的东西和一切匮乏感。"）

- "And simple flavours give as much pleasure as costly fare, when everything that can give pain, and every feeling of want, is removed."
  （"当消除了所有痛苦和匮乏感时，简单的口味提供的快乐与昂贵的食物一样多。"）

- "For it is not continued drinking and revelling, or intercourse with boys and women, or feasts of fish and other such things, as a costly table supplies, that make life pleasant, but sober contemplation."
  （"使生活愉快的不是无休止的酒宴和狂欢，也不是与男女的交合，也不是昂贵的鱼类或其他奢华的宴席，而是清醒的沉思。"）

- "Since this is the aim of living happily; for it is for the sake of this that we do everything, wishing to avoid grief and fear."
  （"因为这是幸福生活的目标；我们做一切事情正是为了这个目的，即避免悲伤和恐惧。"）

- "Prudence is something more valuable than even philosophy."
  （"审慎比哲学本身更有价值。"）

- "He laughs at destiny, whom some have introduced as the mistress of all things."
  （"他嘲笑命运，有些人把命运当作万物的主宰。"）

- "For a man who lives among immortal blessings is not like to a mortal being."
  （"因为一个生活在不朽福祉中的人，不像凡人。"）

---

Source: Diogenes Laertius, Lives of the Philosophers, Book 10, translated by C.D. Yonge (1895)
