# Sources: Britannica, World History Encyclopedia

## Ben Jonson 对莎士比亚的评价
Ben Jonson on Shakespeare: "not of an age, but for all time"
Ben Jonson also called Shakespeare "Sweet Swan of Avon"
Jonson criticized Shakespeare's "small Latin and less Greek"

## 关于莎士比亚真实性的争议
The Shakespeare authorship question has persisted for more than a century. Anti-Stratfordian theories propose alternate authors including Edward de Vere (Earl of Oxford), Francis Bacon, Christopher Marlowe, William Stanley (Earl of Derby), Mary Herbert (Countess of Pembroke), and Emilia Lanier.

Mainstream scholarship largely rejects these theories because:
- Shakespeare's name appears on some 50 extant documents
- He was mentioned by several contemporaries, notably Ben Jonson
- The First Folio (1623), edited by John Heminge and Henry Condell, provides the strongest evidence

## 莎士比亚对英语语言的贡献
- He invented or popularized thousands of words and phrases
- "Bedroom," "lonely," "generous," "accommodation," "barefoot," "eyeball" — all believed to be Shakespeare's coinages
- Phrases like "break the ice," "heart of gold," "wild goose chase," "short shrift" all originate in his works

## 创作风格分析
- 悲剧（Great Tragedies）：Hamlet, Othello, King Lear, Macbeth
- 喜剧（Comedies）：A Midsummer Night's Dream, Twelfth Night, Much Ado About Nothing, The Merchant of Venice
- 历史剧（Histories）：Henry IV, Henry V, Richard III
- 传奇剧/悲喜剧（Tragicomedies）：The Tempest, The Winter's Tale
- Problem Plays: Measure for Measure, Troilus and Cressida, All's Well That Ends Well

## 莎士比亚的影响力
Shakespeare's plays are performed more often than those of any other playwright. His sonnets are still widely read. Modern psychology, philosophy, and literary criticism continue to draw on his insights.

## 关于"失落的岁月"
Shakespeare's whereabouts during the 'lost years' (1585-1592) have been widely discussed:
- Nicholas Rowe (first biographer) claims he was forced to leave Stratford after being caught poaching deer
- Another tradition suggests he became a country schoolmaster
- Some believe he was a soldier in the Low Countries
- John Aubrey claims he was apprenticed to a butcher: "when [Shakespeare] killed a calf, he would do it in high style, and make a speech"

## 遗言与遗产
In his will, Shakespeare left most of his estate to his eldest daughter Susanna, while his wife Anne was left only "my second best bed" — a bequest that has puzzled scholars for centuries.

Shakespeare died on 23 April 1616 at age 52. His birthday is traditionally celebrated on 23 April (same as his death date and believed to be his baptism date of April 26, 1564).
