# 荷马史诗直接引语摘录
# 整理自 World History Encyclopedia - Homer, Iliad, Odyssey

## 史诗开篇（伊利亚特）
"Sing, O goddess, the anger of Achilles son of Peleus, that brought countless ills upon the Achaeans."
— Homer, Iliad, Book 1, Line 1

## 关于战争与死亡
"This is the fate the gods have spun for poor mortal men, that we should live in misery, but they themselves have no sorrows."
— Achilles to Priam, Iliad, Book 24

"Wretched mortals, who are like leaves – for a time they flourish in a blaze of glory, and feed on the yield of the earth, and then again they fade lifeless."
— Apollo to Achilles, Iliad, Book 21

## 关于人类的处境
Achilles: "I would rather work the soil as a serf on hire to some landless impoverished peasant than be King of all these lifeless dead."
— Homer, Odyssey, Book 11

## 关于归家
Nestor: "There was not a man that dared to match his wits against the admirable Odysseus, who in every kind of strategy proved himself supreme."
— Homer, Odyssey, Book 3

## 关于文明与野蛮
Odysseus: "And we came to the land of the Cyclopes, a fierce, lawless people who never lift a hand to plant or plough but just leave everything to the immortal gods."
— Homer, Odyssey, Book 9

## 关于命运的不可抗拒
"The wind that blows from the plain of Troy."
— Homer, Odyssey (passim)

## 关于求婚者的结局
Odysseus: "You dogs! You never thought to see me back from Troy. So you fleeced my household; you raped my maids; you courted my wife behind my back though I was alive...one and all, your fate is sealed."
— Homer, Odyssey, Book 22

## 关于名声与荣耀
Hector: "And people will say, even men of generations not yet born, as they sail by over the sparkling sea in their many-benched ships: 'This is the mound of a man who died long ago. He was the greatest of men, and glorious Hector killed him.'"
— Homer, Iliad, Book 7

## 雅典娜论人类
Athena (to Odysseus in disguise): "...the gods all have their several honours, and death comes to all..."
— Homer, Odyssey

## 关于记忆与遗忘
"The lotus-eaters...who live on that flower."
— Homer, Odyssey, Book 9

## 关于女性的诱惑
Calypso to Odysseus: "Would you indeed be willing to stay here with me and be immortal?"
— Homer, Odyssey, Book 5

## 关于勇气
"He [Odysseus] was sitting disconsolate on the shore in his accustomed place, tormenting himself with tears and sighs and heartache, and looking out across the barren sea with streaming eyes."
— Narrator, Odyssey, Book 5

## 关于神人关系
Zeus: "There is nothing more deadly than man."
— Homer, Odyssey (adapted)

## 普里阿摩斯的求情
Achilles (receiving Priam): "We have had our share of grief, you and I. The gods spun fate for us both..."
— Homer, Iliad, Book 24

## 吊祭文化
"The dead are but bone and dust."
— Homer, Iliad

## 关于智慧
"His [Odysseus'] mind was always teeming with ideas."
— Homer, Odyssey
