# 马尔萨斯语录集

## 关于人口原理

"The power of population is indefinitely greater than the power in the earth to produce subsistence for man."
— An Essay on the Principle of Population, 1798, Chapter 2, p. 8

"If the subsistence for man that the earth affords was to be increased every twenty-five years by a quantity equal to what the whole world at present produces, this would allow the power of production in the earth to be absolutely unlimited... yet still the power of population being a power of a superior order, the increase of the human species can only be kept commensurate to the increase of the means of subsistence by the constant operation of the strong law of necessity acting as a check upon the greater power."
— An Essay on the Principle of Population, 1798, Chapter 2

"Yet in all societies, even those that are most vicious, the tendency to a virtuous attachment [i.e., marriage] is so strong that there is a constant effort towards an increase of population. This constant effort as constantly tends to subject the lower classes of the society to distress and to prevent any great permanent amelioration of their condition."
— An Essay on the Principle of Population, 1798, Chapter II, p. 18

"The increase of population is necessarily limited by subsistence."
— An Essay on the Principle of Population

"population does invariably increase when the means of subsistence increase."
— An Essay on the Principle of Population

"The superior power of population repress by moral restraint, vice, and misery."
— An Essay on the Principle of Population

## 关于济贫法

"If they had 'never existed,' wrote Malthus, 'though there might have been a few more instances of severe distress, the aggregate mass of happiness among the common people would have been much greater than it is at present.'"
— Britannica summary of Malthus's view on Poor Laws

"These laws limited the mobility of labour, he said, and encouraged fecundity and should be abolished. For the most unfortunate it might be reasonable to establish workhouses—not 'comfortable asylums' but places in which 'fare should be hard' and 'severe distress…find some alleviation.'"
— Malthus on Poor Laws

## 关于政治经济学

"the principles of saving, pushed to excess, would destroy the motive to production."
— Malthus, Principles of Political Economy, 1820

"To maximize wealth, a nation had to balance 'the power to produce and the will to consume.'"
— Malthus, Principles of Political Economy, 1820
