# 亚当·斯密引语集
# Sources: Britannica, Wikipedia, World History Encyclopedia, Panmure House

## 关于自身治学经历

1. "By far the most useful and therefore as by far the happiest and most honourable period of my life."
   — Smith wrote this in a letter of thanks for the honour of being elected Rector of Glasgow University in 1787, remembering his professorial days.
   Source: University of Glasgow

2. "In the University of Oxford, the greater part of the public professors have, for these many years, given up altogether even the pretence of teaching."
   — Book V, Chapter II of The Wealth of Nations
   Source: Wikipedia

3. "I have begun to write a book to pass away the time."
   — Smith wrote to Hume from Toulouse, France, during his tutoring tour.
   Source: Wikipedia

## 关于市场与竞争

4. "It is not from the benevolence of the butcher, the brewer, or the baker, that we expect our dinner, but from their regard to their own interest."
   Source: Panmure House / Adam Smith Quotes FAQ

5. "No society can surely be flourishing and happy, of which the far greater part of the members are poor and miserable."
   Source: Panmure House / Adam Smith Quotes FAQ

6. "Civil government, so far as it is instituted for the security of property, is in reality instituted for the defence of the rich against the poor, or of those who have some property against those who have none at all."
   Source: Britannica, The Wealth of Nations

7. "The desire that comes with us from the womb, and never leaves us until we go into the grave."
   — Describing competitive self-betterment drive
   Source: Britannica, The Wealth of Nations

## 关于无形之手

8. "The invisible hand" — the idea that individuals pursuing self-interest unintentionally benefit society.
   Source: World History Encyclopedia

## 关于道德哲学

9. "Smith defined 'mutual sympathy' as the basis of moral sentiments."
   Source: Wikipedia

10. "Smith's man of perfect virtue 'joins to the most perfect command of his own original and selfish feelings, the most exquisite sensibility both to the original and sympathetic feelings of others.'"
    Source: World History Encyclopedia, citing Blackburn

## 关于劳动与价值

11. "In the age of hunters, 'there is scarce any property…; so there is seldom any established magistrate or any regular administration of justice.'"
    Source: Britannica, The Wealth of Nations

12. "With all its imperfections, [the Physiocratic school] is perhaps the nearest approximation to the truth that has yet been published upon the subject of political economy."
    Source: Wikipedia, citing Smith's assessment of Physiocracy

## 关于牛津大学

13. "The Oxford of [Smith's] time gave little if any help towards what was to be his lifework."
    — William Robert Scott
    Source: Wikipedia

14. "He would have made, I fear, a poor gipsy."
    — John Rae commenting on Smith's abduction by Romani at age three
    Source: Britannica
