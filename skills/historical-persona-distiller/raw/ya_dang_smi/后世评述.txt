# 亚当·斯密后世评述

## Britannica 评价
"Adam Smith is a towering figure in the history of economic thought. Known primarily for a single work—An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations (1776)... he is more properly regarded as a social philosopher whose economic writings constitute only the capstone to an overarching view of political and social evolution."

"Smith refuses to explain the distribution of wealth and power in terms of divine will and instead appeals to natural, political, social, economic, legal, environmental and technological factors."

## World History Encyclopedia 评价
"Regarded incorrectly as a champion of laissez-faire economics, Smith supported state intervention in important areas like the education of workers."

"Smith is realistic, unsentimental, and optimistic in his determination to improve the human condition. He believes in progress."

## 经济学史评价
"Smith proposed the labour theory of value based on this interconnectedness between humans in the world of trade."

"The Wealth of Nations opens with a famous passage on the division of labour in a pin factory."

"Smith was also determined to show in his book that the Christian dislike of wealth had no foundation in nature and no place in modern economics."

## 思想史比较
"There is an obvious resemblance between this succession of changes in the material basis of production... and the Marxian conception of history. Although the resemblance is indeed remarkable, there is also a crucial difference: in the Marxian scheme the engine of evolution is ultimately the struggle between contending socioeconomic classes, whereas in Smith's philosophical history the primal moving agency is 'human nature' driven by the desire for self-betterment."
— Britannica

## 关于亚当·斯密的教育风格
"Smith was known to dislike students who took copious lecture notes, or 'scribblers' as he called them, and encouraged his students to listen and reflect on the lecture's content instead."
— University of Glasgow
