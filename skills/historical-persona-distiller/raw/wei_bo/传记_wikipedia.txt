# Max Weber 生平 (来源: Wikipedia)

## 出生与早年
Maximilian Carl Emil Weber (1864年4月21日-1920年6月14日) was a German sociologist, historian, jurist, and political economist who was one of the central figures in the development of sociology and the social sciences more generally. His ideas continue to influence social theory and research.

Born in Erfurt in 1864, Weber studied law and history in Berlin, Göttingen, and Heidelberg. His father Max Weber Sr. held posts as a lawyer, civil servant, and parliamentarian for the National Liberal Party in the Prussian Landtag and German Reichstag. This immersed his home in both politics and academia. His mother Helene Fallenstein was partly descended from the French Huguenot Souchay family, which had obtained wealth through international commerce and the textile industry. Over time, Weber was affected by the marital and personality tensions between his father, who enjoyed material pleasures while overlooking religious and philanthropic causes, and his mother, a devout Calvinist and philanthropist.

Before graduating from the Kaiserin-Augusta-Gymnasium in 1882, Weber secretly read all forty volumes of Johann Wolfgang von Goethe's works. He read many other classical works, including those by the philosopher Immanuel Kant. For Christmas in 1877, a thirteen-year-old Weber gifted his parents two historical essays.

## 学术生涯与崩溃
Weber enrolled in Heidelberg University in 1882 as a law student. In 1886, he passed the Referendar legal training examination. Under the tutelage of Levin Goldschmidt and Rudolf von Gneist, Weber wrote his dissertation in 1889. His habilitation was completed in 1891.

He married his second cousin Marianne Schnitger on September 20, 1893. Marianne was a feminist activist and an author in her own right. They had no children. Academically, between completing his dissertation and habilitation, Weber became interested in contemporary social policy and joined the Verein für Sozialpolitik in 1888.

He was appointed professor of economics at the University of Freiburg in 1894. His 1895 inaugural lecture, "The Nation State and Economic Policy," criticised Polish immigration and argued that the Junkers encouraged it to serve their economic interests over those of Germany. It influenced the politician Friedrich Naumann to create the National-Social Association.

In June 1897, Weber had a severe quarrel with his father over his father's treatment of his mother. Weber Sr. died two months later during a trip to Riga, leaving the argument unresolved. Afterwards, Weber became increasingly depressed, nervous, and insomniac, making it difficult for him to fulfill his professorial duties. He sought a teaching exemption, which was granted in 1899. He fully withdrew from teaching in 1903 and did not return to it until 1918.

## 恢复与学术重出
After recovering from his illness, Weber became an associate editor of the Archiv für Sozialwissenschaft und Sozialpolitik in 1904. He published some of his most seminal works in it, including his book The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism, which became his most famous work.

Also in 1904, he participated in the Congress of Arts and Sciences that was part of the World's Fair in St. Louis. Taking advantage of it, the Webers travelled throughout the country for almost three months in a round trip that began and ended in New York City. He learned more about America's social, economic, and theological conditions and their relationship with his thesis.

In 1909, having become increasingly dissatisfied with the political conservatism and perceived lack of methodological discipline of the Verein, he co-founded the German Sociological Association (Deutsche Gesellschaft für Soziologie) and was its first treasurer. He resigned from his position as treasurer in 1912. His support for value-freedom in the social sciences caused that, as it was a controversial position in the association.

## 一战与政治参与
After the First World War began in 1914, Weber volunteered for service and was appointed as a reserve officer in charge of organising the Heidelberg army hospitals. Early on, he strongly supported the German war effort. However, Weber later became one of the most prominent critics of both German expansionism and the Kaiser's war policies. He publicly criticised Germany's potential annexation of Belgium and unrestricted submarine warfare, later supporting calls for constitutional reform, democratisation, and universal suffrage.

## 魏玛时期与晚年
When defeat came in 1918, Germany found in Weber a public intellectual leader with unscathed liberal credentials. He was invited to join the draft board of the Weimar Constitution as well as the German delegation to Versailles. He opposed the German Revolution and the Versailles Treaty alike.

In 1919, he briefly taught at the universities of Vienna and Munich (where he gave the much-lauded lectures, "Science as a Vocation" and "Politics as a Vocation").

He died of pneumonia in 1920 at the age of 56, possibly as a result of the post-war Spanish flu pandemic. A book, Economy and Society, was left unfinished.

## 主要著作
- "The 'Objectivity' of Knowledge in Social Science and Social Policy" (1904)
- The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism (1904–1905)
- The Economic Ethics of the World Religions (1915–1921)
- "Science as a Vocation" (1917)
- "Politics as a Vocation" (1919)
- Economy and Society (1921–1922)
- General Economic History (1923)
