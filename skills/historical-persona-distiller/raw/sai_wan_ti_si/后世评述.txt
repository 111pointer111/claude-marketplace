# 塞万提斯（Cervantes）后世评述
# PID: sai_wan_ti_si

================================================================================
一、Britannica 评述
================================================================================

"the most important and celebrated figure in Spanish literature"
——Britannica 对塞万提斯的评价
原文：Miguel de Cervantes... was a Spanish novelist, playwright, and poet, the
creator of Don Quixote (1605, 1615) and the most important and celebrated
figure in Spanish literature.

"the figures of Don Quixote and Sancho Panza are probably familiar visually
to more people than any other imaginary characters in world literature."
——Britannica
原文：owing to their widespread representation in art, drama, and film, the
figures of Don Quixote and Sancho Panza are probably familiar visually to more
people than any other imaginary characters in world literature.

"Cervantes was a great experimenter. He tried his hand in all the major
literary genres save the epic."
——Britannica

"a few of those in his collection of Novelas exemplares (1613; Exemplary Stories)
attain a level close to that of Don Quixote, on a miniature scale."
——Britannica

"critical discussion of the work has proceeded unabated since the 18th century."
——Britannica谈《堂吉诃德》

"the most adventurous period of Cervantes's life, supplied subject matter for
several of his literary works, notably the Captive's tale in Don Quixote and
the two Algiers plays."
——Britannica谈阿尔及尔囚禁经历对写作的影响

================================================================================
二、"第一部现代小说"——多方认可
================================================================================

"Literary historians consider Don Quixote one of the most important books of
all time, and often cite it as the first modern novel."
——Interlude.hk

"Since his passing, Cervantes has been credited with writing the first modern
novel."
——Biography.com

"the first modern novel"
——被多来源广泛引用的评价

================================================================================
三、"世界第一本畅销书"
================================================================================

"Don Quixote became the world's first best seller and was eventually translated
into more than 60 different languages."
——Biography.com

"Editions continue regularly to be printed, and critical discussion of the
work has proceeded unabated since the 18th century."
——Britannica

"His novel Don Quixote has been translated, in full or in part, into more than
60 languages."
——Britannica

================================================================================
四、"西班牙社会的辉煌全景图"
================================================================================

"he ended by expanding his work into a brilliant panorama of Spanish society
as it existed during the 16th century."
——Interlude.hk，引自对《堂吉诃德》的文学批评

"A good many critics regard Don Quixote 'as a symbolic, didactic or controversial
work intended to bring about radical reforms in church and state.'"
——Interlude.hk，引述批评界观点（但同时指出塞万提斯本人未必有此意图）

================================================================================
五、西格蒙德·弗洛伊德学西班牙语读塞万提斯
================================================================================

"English writers rediscovered Cervantes in the mid-18th century and Sigmund
Freud claimed to have learned Spanish by reading Cervantes in the original
language."
——Interlude.hk

================================================================================
六、对后世作家的深远影响
================================================================================

"His work has inspired countless other authors throughout the centuries—
including Gustave Flaubert, Henry Fielding and Fyodor Dostoyevsky"
——Biography.com

具体影响：
- 居斯塔夫·福楼拜（Gustave Flaubert）
- 亨利·菲尔丁（Henry Fielding）
- 费奥多尔·陀思妥耶夫斯基（Fyodor Dostoyevsky）
——以上三位作家均受塞万提斯影响

"the story of Don Quixote has been retold in many ways, including in the
popular musical The Man of La Mancha and in an artwork by Pablo Picasso."
——Biography.com

================================================================================
七、其他重要后世评价
================================================================================

"Cervantes simply wasn't able to make a living from literature, and instead
earned his money collecting taxes and trading in grains."
——Interlude.hk，描述塞万提斯现实中的困顿

"Despite its undisputed place in the literary canon, Don Quixote did not make
Cervantes wealthy at the time, as authors did not receive royalties for their
works."
——Biography.com

"He was buried on the grounds of a convent there, in an unmarked grave."
——Biography.com，描述塞万提斯身后的萧索

"the word quixotic... entered common usage."
——Interlude.hk，"quixotic"一词进入日常语言

"the character of Quixote became an archetype"
——Interlude.hk，堂吉诃德成为文学原型

"Galatea was a pastoral novel, and according to critics 'exhibits a certain
power of inventiveness and some depth of real emotion.'"
——Interlude.hk，评论《加拉苔亚》

"English writers rediscovered Cervantes in the mid-18th century"
——Interlude.hk，英国作家在18世纪中叶重新发现塞万提斯
