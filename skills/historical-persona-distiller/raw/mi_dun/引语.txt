# 弥尔顿 John Milton 代表作品原文引用

## Paradise Lost 引语

### 1. 开篇Invocation (Book I, lines 1-26)
"Sing Heav'nly Muse, that on the secret top
Of Horeb, or of Sinai, didst inspire
That shepherd, who first taught the chosen seed,
In the beginning how the heav'ns and earth
Rose out of chaos; or if Sion hill
Delight thee more, and Siloa's brook that flowed
Fast by the oracle of God: I thence
Invoke thy aid to my advent'rous song,
That with no middle flight intends to soar
Above the Aonian mount, while it pursues
Things unattempted yet in prose or rhyme."

### 2. 撒旦主题"The mind is its own place" (Book I, lines 253-258)
"The mind is its own place, and in itself
Can make a Heaven of Hell, a Hell of Heaven.
What matter where, if I be still the same?"

### 3. 人类堕落主题"Paradise Lost" (Book I, lines 1-5)
"Of Man's first disobedience, and the fruit
Of that forbidden tree whose mortal taste
Brought death into the World, and all our woe,
With loss of Eden, till one greater Man
Restore us, and regain the blissful seat."

### 4. 撒旦的骄傲"The Unconquerable Will" (Book I, lines 106-110)
"What though the mere giddie round
He change his high estate, and high decrees?
Chiefe of the Golden throng? and at his will
Thogh in the Warlike Garden of Milan, nothing danger'd?
The Will, and so much Righteousness of God?"

### 5. 基督英雄主义Christian Heroism - the Son's self-sacrifice
"...the Son is more heroic because he is willing to undergo voluntary humiliation, a sign of his consummate love for humankind. He foreknows that he will become incarnate in order to suffer death, a selfless act whereby humankind will be redeemed."

### 6. "All is not lost" (Book I, lines 106-110)
"All is not lost; the unconquerable will,
And study of revenge, immortal hate,
And the courage never to submit or yield."

### 7. 天使之战描述 (Book VI)
The battle between the good and evil angels; the defeat of the latter results in their expulsion from heaven.

### 8. 弥尔顿的目的 "justify the ways of God to man" (Book I)
"To justify the ways of God to men"

### 9. 撒旦的史诗比喻
"His shield / Like as the Moon..." (Book I, lines 287-297)
"Thick as autumnal leaves that strow the brooks / In Vallombrosa"

### 10. 失乐园主题
"Here Nature first begins / To prove her growth, and with assaying arms / Tempts the forbidden Tree."

## Paradise Regained 引语

### 11. 诱惑主题
Paradise Regained dramatizes how Jesus embodies Christian heroism through resisting temptations that pander to one's impulses toward ease, pleasure, worldliness, and power.

### 12. 耶稣的辩论
"In effect, Paradise Regained unfolds as a series of debates—an ongoing dialectic—in which Jesus analyzes and refutes Satan's arguments. With clarity and cogency, Jesus rebuts any and all arguments by using recta ratio, always informed by faith in God, his father."

## Samson Agonistes 引语

### 13. 参孙的悲剧
"Samson Agonistes" dramatizes the violence of despair and the possibility of resurrection through suffering.

## Areopagitica 引语

### 14. 出版自由
"Give me the liberty to know, to utter, and to argue freely according to conscience, above all liberties."

## Lycidas 引语

### 15. 悲悼主题
Lycidas是弥尔顿1637年为悼念亡友爱德华·金而写的著名哀歌，开创了英诗中的"哀歌"传统。

## 关键主题句

### 16. 自由与责任
"Better to reign in Hell than serve in Heaven." — Paradise Lost, Book I

### 17. 知识与堕落
"Knowledge is the precise outcome of unhappy experience, which makes Eve take the fruit." — Paradise Lost interpretation

### 18. 赎罪主题
"Despite Satan's success against Adam and Eve, the hope of regeneration after sinfulness is provided by the Son's self-sacrifice."
