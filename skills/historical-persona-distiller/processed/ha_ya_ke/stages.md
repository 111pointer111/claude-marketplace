## Stage 2 — Life Phases

### Phase 1: Viennese Formation (1899–1921)
- Born into intellectual family in Vienna
- Father: Dr. August von Hayek (botany professor)
- Wittgenstein was second cousin; mother played with Wittgenstein sisters
- Poor student, bottom of class in Latin, Greek, mathematics
- Interested in theater, biology; read Goethe, Darwin, Aristotle
- WWI service (artillery, Italian front); damaged hearing

### Phase 2: Conversion from Socialism (1921–1931)
- Initially sympathetic to democratic socialism, until ~age 23
- Read Mises' "Socialism" (1922): "To none of us young men who read the book when it appeared, the world was ever the same again"
- Attended Mises' Privatseminar; befriended Haberler, Machlup, Morgenstern, etc.
- Earned two doctorates (law 1921, political science 1923)
- Research assistant at NYU under Jeremiah Jenks
- Founded Austrian Institute for Business Cycle Research (1927–1931)

### Phase 3: LSE Ascendancy and the Keynes Debate (1931–1950)
- Took Tooke Chair at LSE in 1931 at Lionel Robbins' invitation
- Published Prices and Production (1931); became leading economic theorist
- Exchanged letters with Keynes (1932); Keynes called his work "frightful muddles"
- Taught future giants: Coase, Galbraith, Kaldor, Lerner, etc.
- Founded Mont Pelerin Society (1947) with Friedman, Popper, etc.
- Published The Road to Serfdom (1944); became hugely influential

### Phase 4: American and German Years (1950–1977)
- Joined University of Chicago Committee on Social Thought (1950–1962)
- Salary funded by William Volker Fund
- Completed The Constitution of Liberty (published 1960)
- Professor at University of Freiburg (1962–1968)
- Professor at University of Salzburg (1969–1977)
- "I made a mistake in moving to Salzburg" — library facilities inadequate

### Phase 5: Nobel Recognition and Final Influence (1974–1992)
- Awarded Nobel Memorial Prize in Economics 1974 (with Myrdal)
- Surprised; believed paired to balance political spectrum
- Award Speech: warned of danger prize authority lends to economist
- Became intellectual godfather to Thatcher; Thatcher held up The Constitution of Liberty at Conservative meeting (1975)
- Presidential Medal of Freedom 1991
- Died March 23, 1992 in Freiburg
