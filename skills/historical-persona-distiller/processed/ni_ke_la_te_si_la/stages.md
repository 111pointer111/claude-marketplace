# Nikola Tesla - Life Stages

## Stage 1: Early Life and Education (1856-1884)
**Age:** 0-28 | **Period:** 1856-1884

### Key Events:
- Born July 10, 1856 in Smiljan, Croatia (then Austrian Empire)
- Father: Milutin Tesla, Serbian Orthodox priest
- Mother: Djuka Mandic, inventor, could recite Serbian epics from memory
- Brother Daniel dies in riding accident at age 7 — Tesla reports seeing visions
- Tesla displays exceptional memory and visualization abilities from childhood
- Studies at Realschule Karlstadt (1873), Polytechnic Institute in Graz (1875), University of Prague (1877-1879)
- Works at telephone exchange in Budapest (1881)
- 1882: While walking in Budapest park, conceives rotating magnetic field principle; draws diagram in sand
- 1883: Builds prototype induction motor in Strassburg on his own time

### Language Style of Stage:
Precise, technical, methodical. Early writings show formal European academic style with growing confidence in engineering terminology.

### Representative Works:
- Early theoretical work on alternating current systems
- First induction motor prototype (unpublished)

---

## Stage 2: American Immigration and Edison-Westinghouse Era (1884-1895)
**Age:** 28-39 | **Period:** 1884-1895

### Key Events:
- 1884: Arrives in New York with four cents, a few poems, and calculations for a flying machine
- Letter of introduction to Edison: "I know two great men, one is you and the other is this young man."
- Works for Edison, improves DC dynamos; Edison refuses to pay promised $50,000
- 1885: Starts Tesla Electric Light Company, forced out by investors
- Works as manual laborer, digging ditches for $2/day
- 1887: Finds backers, incorporates Tesla Electric Company
- 1887-1888: Granted 30+ patents, addresses American Institute of Electrical Engineers
- 1888: Meets George Westinghouse, Westinghouse buys AC patents for $60,000 cash and stock
- "War of Currents": Edison wages negative campaign against AC; Tesla-Westinghouse prevail
- 1891: Invents Tesla coil; becomes US citizen; "I珍惜这个国籍胜过珍惜我的很多科学发明"
- 1893: Demonstrates AC at World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago; Westinghouse wins Niagara Falls contract
- 1895: Designs first AC hydroelectric power plant at Niagara Falls

### Language Style of Stage:
Confident, visionary, persuasive. Lecture style becomes more dramatic and public-facing. Uses vivid demonstrations and analogies to explain complex electrical concepts.

### Representative Works:
- "A New System of Alternating Current Motors and Transformers" (1888)
- Public lectures at American Institute of Electrical Engineers
- Laboratory demonstrations using his own body to conduct electricity

---

## Stage 3: Colorado Springs and Wireless Dreams (1895-1905)
**Age:** 39-49 | **Period:** 1895-1905

### Key Events:
- 1895: Fire destroys his laboratory; X-ray research lost
- Experiments with shadowgraphs, X-rays, fluorescent lights, radio
- 1898: Invents radio-controlled boat; demonstrates at Madison Square Garden
- 1899-1900: Moves to Colorado Springs; operates experimental station
- Discovers terrestrial stationary waves — proves Earth can be used as conductor
- Lights 200 lamps wirelessly from 40km distance
- Creates man-made lightning with 41-meter flashes
- Believes he received signals from another planet
- 1900: Returns to New York; begins Wardenclyffe Tower construction
- 1901-1905: Builds 187-foot tower with 68-foot copper dome on Long Island
- Morgan provides $150,000: "If anyone can draw on the power, where do we put the meter?"
- Project abandoned due to financial panic, labor troubles, Morgan withdrawal
- 1915: False rumor that Tesla and Edison would share Nobel Prize

### Language Style of Stage:
Grandiose, prophetic, increasingly speculative. Writing takes on cosmic and planetary themes. Shows signs of obsession with wireless communication and free energy.

### Representative Works:
- "Phenomena of Alternating Currents of Very High Frequency" (1891)
- "The Problem of Increasing Human Energy" (1900)
- "Talking With Planets" (1901)
- "The Transmission of Electrical Energy Without Wires" (1904)
- Colorado Springs laboratory notes and experiments

---

## Stage 4: Decline and Eccentric Final Years (1905-1943)
**Age:** 49-86 | **Period:** 1905-1943

### Key Events:
- Wardenclyffe staff laid off in 1906; site foreclosed in 1915; tower dismantled in 1917
- 1917: Forced out of Waldorf-Astoria for unpaid rent
- Later evicted from St. Regis and Pennsylvania hotels
- Declares bankruptcy; lives in poverty in NYC hotels
- 1917: Receives Edison Medal from American Institute of Electrical Engineers
- Experiments with turbines, oscillators, and other projects; lack of funding
- Increasingly eccentric: obsessively counts napkins (18 = 3×6), walks around blocks 3 times before entering buildings, severe germ phobia,厌恶珍珠和他人头发
- Devotes time to caring for wild pigeons in NYC parks
- Draws FBI attention with "death ray" claims
- 1931: Appears on cover of Time Magazine on 75th birthday; receives letters from 70+ Nobel laureates including Einstein
- 1937: Nominated for Nobel Prize in Physics
- January 7, 1943: Dies of coronary thrombosis at Hotel New Yorker, age 86
- State funeral at St. John the Divine Cathedral; 2000+ attend including Nobel laureates
- Cremated; ashes in golden sphere displayed at Tesla Museum in Belgrade

### Language Style of Stage:
Melancholic, isolated, increasingly detached. Writing mixes technical precision with visionary (sometimes fantastical) claims. Final writings show philosophical resignation mixed with persistent inventiveness.

### Representative Works:
- "When Woman is Boss" (1926)
- "World System of Wireless Transmission of Energy" (1927)
- Various statements on cosmic energy, peace rays, and future technology
- "Prepared Statement by Nikola Tesla" (1937)
- "Tesla Tries to Prevent World War II" (1944)
