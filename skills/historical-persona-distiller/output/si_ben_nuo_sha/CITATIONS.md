## 一、传记原文引用

### 1. 斯宾诺莎传（home.yulei.org，俞磊，2007年5月4日）

> "别涅狄克特·斯宾诺莎（Benedict de Spinoza，这是拉丁文名字，他的希伯莱文原名：Baruch Spinoza），1631年11月24日出生于荷兰阿姆斯特丹。他的祖先是从西班牙或葡萄牙迁来的犹太人。斯宾诺莎的父亲迈克尔（Michael）比较富有而且在当地犹太人社区中有一定的声望。"

> "斯宾诺莎少年时就读于阿姆斯特丹犹太人自办的一所学校……斯宾诺莎有幸遇到了两位老师：一位名叫马纳塞·本·伊兹拉亚（Manassch ben Israel），另一位名叫法兰西斯·凡·登·恩登（Francis van den Enden）"

> "1656年，犹太教的法庭传训了他，并开除了他的教籍。犹太教会还要求阿姆斯特丹市政当局驱逐斯宾诺莎。斯宾诺莎的回答是：'很好，这样我就不必强迫我去做我本意所不愿意做的任何事情了'。"

> "1661年，在《知性改进论》中他写下这一段文字：'当我受到经验的教训之后,才深悟得日常生活中习见的一切东西，都是虚幻的、无谓的。……那些在生活中最常见，并且由人们的行为所表明，被当作是最高幸福的东西，归纳起来，大约不外三项：财富、荣誉和感官快乐。……我彻底下决心，放弃迷乱人心的财富、荣誉、肉体快乐这三种东西，则我所放弃的必定是真正的恶，而我所得到的必定是真正的善。'"

> "斯宾诺莎在莱因斯堡只住了三年……莱因斯堡距离著名的莱顿大学只有六七公里……斯宾诺莎所住的那所小房子（现在已经是斯宾诺莎博物馆）成了许多教授们经常光顾的地方。"

> "1663年6月，斯宾诺莎移居伏尔堡……惠更斯1664年至1666年就住在斯宾诺莎附近，因此两人有着频繁的接触，大家都从事磨制镜片的工作。"

> "1672年，法国路易十四却与英国联合，共同对荷兰宣战，十二万法国大军的入侵使荷兰面对空前的危机。……1672年8月，德·韦特在大街上为群起的暴民所杀害。……哲学家愤怒了，斯宾诺莎激动地要来到街上张贴公告，怒斥暴民的行为。他的朋友们把他拦住，才使他没有与德·韦特同归与尽。"

> "也许是磨制镜片吸入的尘埃所致，斯宾诺莎患上了严重肺病，1677年2月21日，年仅四十四岁哲学家就离开了人世。"

> "斯宾诺莎死后一百多年，直到1780年前后，德国作家莱辛在和思想家耶科比开始重新发现他，此后斯宾诺莎在德国思想界开始闻名。……黑格尔宣称：'要达到斯宾诺莎的哲学成就是不容易的，要达到斯宾诺莎的人格是不可能的。'诗人海涅这样形容斯宾诺莎对后来哲学的影响：'所有我们现代的哲学家，都是透过斯宾诺莎磨制的眼镜来看世界的。'"

## 二、Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy 原文引用

> "Bento (in Hebrew, Baruch; in Latin, Benedictus: all three names mean 'blessed') Spinoza is one of the most important philosophers—and certainly the most radical—of the early modern period."

> "On July 27, 1656, Spinoza was issued the harshest writ of herem, ban or excommunication, ever pronounced by the Sephardic community of Amsterdam; it was never rescinded."

> "When Spinoza died in 1677, in The Hague, he was still at work on his Political Treatise; this was soon published by his friends along with his other unpublished writings."

### Ethics 原文引用

**关于实体定义：**
> "By substance I understand what is in itself and is conceived through itself"
> "By attribute I understand what the intellect perceives of a substance, as constituting its essence"
> "By God I understand a being absolutely infinite, i.e., a substance consisting of an infinity of attributes, of which each one expresses an eternal and infinite essence."

**关于上帝唯一性（Ip14）：**
> "Except God, no substance can be or be conceived."

**关于一切在上帝之中（Ip15）：**
> "Whatever is, is in God, and nothing can be or be conceived without God."

**关于自然必然性（Ip17s1）：**
> "all things have necessarily flowed, or always followed, by the same necessity and in the same way as from the nature of a triangle it follows, from eternity and to eternity, that its three angles are equal to two right angles"

**关于无偶然（Ip29）：**
> "In nature there is nothing contingent, but all things have been determined from the necessity of the divine nature to exist and produce an effect in a certain way."

**关于神迹之不可能性（I, Appendix）：**
> "God does not perform miracles, since there are no, and cannot be, departures whatsoever from the necessary course of nature."

**关于身心同一（IIp7s）：**
> "a mode of extension and the idea of that mode are one and the same thing, but expressed in two ways"
> "the order and connection of ideas is the same as the order and connection of things"

**关于真理定义（IIp43s）：**
> "A true idea means nothing other than knowing a thing perfectly, or in the best way"

**关于理性把握必然（IIp44）：**
> "It is of the nature of Reason to regard things as necessary, not as contingent. And Reason perceives this necessity of things truly, i.e., as it is in itself. But this necessity of things is the very necessity of God's eternal nature. Therefore, it is of the nature of Reason to regard things under this species of eternity"

**关于人类无意志自由（IIp48）：**
> "In the Mind there is no absolute, or free, will, but the Mind is determined to will this or that by a cause that is also determined by another, and this again by another, and so to infinity"

**关于被动情感（III, Preface）：**
> "We are driven about in many ways by external causes, and … like waves on the sea, driven by contrary winds, we toss about, not knowing our outcome and fate"

**关于宗教批判（I, Appendix）：**
> "One who seeks the true causes of miracles, and is eager, like an educated man, to understand natural things, not to wonder at them, like a fool, is generally considered and denounced as an impious heretic by those whom the people honor as interpreters of nature and the Gods. For they know that if ignorance is taken away, then foolish wonder, the only means they have of arguing and defending their authority is also taken away."

**关于自然无目的论（I, Appendix）：**
> "Nature has no end set before it … All things proceed by a certain eternal necessity of nature."

**关于Deus sive Natura（Part IV, Preface）：**
> "That eternal and infinite being we call God, or Nature, acts from the same necessity from which he exists"
