## 主要原文来源

### 1. Letter to Menoeceus（致美诺西乌斯书）

**来源**：MIT Internet Classics Archive
**URL**：https://classics.mit.edu/Epicurus/menoec.html
**译者**：Robert Drew Hicks（1925）
**访问时间**：2026-04-30

**引用条目**：

1. "Let no one be slow to seek wisdom when he is young nor weary in the search thereof when he is grown old."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

2. "For truly there are gods, and knowledge of them is evident; but they are not such as the multitude believe..."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

3. "Not the person who denies the gods worshipped by the multitude, but he who affirms of the gods what the multitude believes about them is truly impious."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

4. "Accustom yourself to believe that death is nothing to us, for good and evil imply awareness, and death is the privation of all awareness..."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

5. "The most formidable of evils, death, is nothing to us, since, when we exist, death is not present to us; and when death is present, then we have no existence."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

6. "of desires some are natural, others are groundless; and that of the natural some are necessary as well as natural, and some natural only..."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

7. "we call pleasure the alpha and omega of a happy life. Pleasure is our first and kindred good."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

8. "By pleasure we mean the absence of pain in the body and of trouble in the soul."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

9. "Of all this the beginning and the greatest good is prudence. For this reason prudence is a more precious thing even than philosophy."
   — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

10. "It teaches us that it is not possible to live pleasantly without living prudently and honorably and justly..."
    — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

11. "Plain fare gives as much pleasure as a costly diet, when one the pain of want has been removed."
    — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

12. "Whatever is natural is easily procured and only the vain and worthless hard to win."
    — Epicurus, Letter to Menoeceus

### 2. Diogenes Laertius, Lives of the Philosophers, Book X

**来源**：attalus.org
**URL**：https://www.attalus.org/old/diogenes10c.html
**译者**：C.D. Yonge（1895）
**访问时间**：2026-04-30

**引用条目**：

1. "We call pleasure the beginning and end of the blessed life. For we recognize pleasure as the first good innate in us..."
   — Diogenes Laertius quoting Epicurus, Book X

2. "Friendship arises from one's wants; but it must be begun on our side. For we sow the earth; and friendship arises from a community of, and participation in, pleasures."
   — Diogenes Laertius quoting Epicurus, Book X

3. "Friendship is the greatest of all goods that wisdom provides for the blessed life."
   — Diogenes Laertius quoting Epicurus, Principal Doctrines

4. "The magnitude of pleasure reaches its limit in the removal of all pain."
   — Diogenes Laertius quoting Epicurus, Principal Doctrines

5. "Nature's wealth at once is limited and easy to procure; but the wealth of idle fancies is without limit and hard to reach."
   — Diogenes Laertius quoting Epicurus, Principal Doctrines
