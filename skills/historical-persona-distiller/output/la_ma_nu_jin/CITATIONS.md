# CITATIONS.md — 拉马努金原文引用清单

## 直接引语（均从原始资料提取，未自行创作）

### 1. "An equation for me has no meaning unless it expresses a thought of God."
**来源：** Ramanujan, quoted in Robert Kanigel, *The Man Who Knew Infinity: A Life of the Genius Ramanujan* (1991)
**背景：** Britannica中也引用——"For him, each theorem was a step closer to understanding the infinite, aligning his mathematical work with a higher spiritual purpose."
**原文所在位置：** Britannica - "Beyond mathematics, Ramanujan delved into the philosophical underpinnings of his work, building a personal theory of reality centered on zero and infinity."

### 2. "He believed numbers and their relationships were not human constructs but divine revelations, offering insights into the universe's mysteries."
**来源：** Britannica - *Srinivasa Ramanujan biography*
**原文：** "Beyond mathematics, Ramanujan delved into the philosophical underpinnings of his work, building a personal theory of reality centered on zero and infinity. He viewed zero as representing absolute reality and infinity as the infinite manifestations of that reality. He believed numbers and their relationships were not human constructs but divine revelations, offering insights into the universe's mysteries."

### 3. "On the other hand he had only a vague idea of what constitutes a mathematical proof."
**来源：** Wikipedia - *Srinivasa Ramanujan*
**原文：** "The letters Ramanujan wrote to Hardy in 1913 had contained many fascinating results. Ramanujan worked out the Riemann series, the elliptic integrals, hypergeometric series and functional equations of the zeta function. On the other hand he had only a vague idea of what constitutes a mathematical proof."

### 4. "Although he was almost completely unaware of modern developments in mathematics, his mastery of continued fractions was unequaled by any living mathematician."
**来源：** Britannica - *Srinivasa Ramanujan biography*
**原文：** "In 1911 Ramanujan published the first of his papers in the Journal of the Indian Mathematical Society. His knowledge of mathematics, most of which he had developed independently, was startling. Although he was almost completely unaware of modern developments in mathematics, his mastery of continued fractions was unequaled by any living mathematician."

### 5. "The limitations of his knowledge were as startling as its profundity."
**来源：** Hardy, quoted in O. Ore, *Biography in Dictionary of Scientific Biography* (New York 1970-1990)
**原文：** St Andrews引述——"What was to be done in the way of teaching him modern mathematics? The limitations of his knowledge were as startling as its profundity."

### 6. "I have had no university education but I have undergone the ordinary school course. After leaving school I have been employing the spare time at my disposal to work at mathematics. I have not trodden through the conventional regular course which is followed in a university course, but I am striking out a new path for myself."
**来源：** Ramanujan's letter to G.H. Hardy, January 1913, quoted in P.K. Srinivasan, *Ramanujan: An Inspiration* (Madras, 1968)
**原文：** St Andrews - "In January 1913 Ramanujan wrote to G H Hardy having seen a copy of his 1910 book Orders of infinity. In Ramanujan's letter to Hardy he introduced himself and his work: 'I have had no university education but I have undergone the ordinary school course. After leaving school I have been employing the spare time at my disposal to work at mathematics. I have not trodden through the conventional regular course which is followed in a university course, but I am striking out a new path for myself.'"

### 7. "I have made a special investigation of divergent series in general and the results I get are termed by the local mathematicians as 'startling'."
**来源：** Ramanujan's letter to G.H. Hardy, January 1913
**原文：** St Andrews - "I have made a special investigation of divergent series in general and the results I get are termed by the local mathematicians as 'startling'."

### 8. "I am already a half starving man. To preserve my brains I want food and this is my first consideration."
**来源：** Ramanujan's letter to Hardy, February 1913, quoted in *S. Ramanujan, Collected Papers* (Cambridge, 1927)
**原文：** St Andrews - "Ramanujan was delighted with Hardy's reply and when he wrote again he said: 'I have found a friend in you who views my labour sympathetically. ... I am already a half starving man. To preserve my brains I want food and this is my first consideration.'"

### 9. "I have found a friend in you who views my labour sympathetically."
**来源：** Ramanujan's letter to Hardy, February 1913, *Collected Papers* (1927)
**原文：** St Andrews - "Ramanujan was delighted with Hardy's reply and when he wrote again he said 'I have found a friend in you who views my labour sympathetically.'"

### 10. "He was miserably poor. ... He said he wanted a pittance to live on so that he might pursue his researches."
**来源：** R. Ramachandra Rao, *In memoriam S. Ramanujan, B.A., F.R.S.*, J. Indian Math. Soc. 12 (1920), 87-90
**原文：** St Andrews - "He writes: 'A short uncouth figure, stout, unshaven, not over clean, with one conspicuous feature – shining eyes – walked in with a frayed notebook under his arm. He was miserably poor. ... He opened his book and began to explain some of his discoveries. ... I asked him what he wanted. He said he wanted a pittance to live on so that he might pursue his researches.'"

### 11. "I have passed the Matriculation Examination and studied up to the First Arts but was prevented from pursuing my studies further owing to several untoward circumstances."
**来源：** Ramanujan's letter of application to Madras Port Trust, February 1912
**原文：** St Andrews - "In 1912 Ramanujan applied for the post of clerk in the accounts section of the Madras Port Trust. In his letter of application he wrote: 'I have passed the Matriculation Examination and studied up to the First Arts but was prevented from pursuing my studies further owing to several untoward circumstances. I have, however, been devoting all my time to Mathematics and developing the subject.'"

### 12. "I can strongly recommend the applicant. He is a young man of quite exceptional capacity in mathematics and especially in work relating to numbers."
**来源：** E.W. Middlemast, Professor of Mathematics at Presidency College, Madras, recommendation letter, 1912
**原文：** St Andrews - "Middlemast, a graduate of St John's College, Cambridge, wrote: 'I can strongly recommend the applicant. He is a young man of quite exceptional capacity in mathematics and especially in work relating to numbers. He has a natural aptitude for computation and is very quick at figure work.'"

### 13. "I was exceedingly interested by your letter and by the theorems which you state."
**来源：** G.H. Hardy's reply to Ramanujan, February 8, 1913, quoted in *B.C. Berndt and R.A. Rankin, Ramanujan: Letters and commentary* (Providence, Rhode Island, 1995)
**原文：** St Andrews - "On 8 February he replied to Ramanujan, the letter beginning: 'I was exceedingly interested by your letter and by the theorems which you state. You will however understand that, before I can judge properly of the value of what you have done, it is essential that I should see proofs of some of your assertions.'"

### 14. "Without an FA degree, he left college and continued to pursue independent research in mathematics, living in extreme poverty and often on the brink of starvation."
**来源：** Wikipedia - *Srinivasa Ramanujan*
**原文：** Wikipedia正文——"Ramanujan failed his Fellow of Arts exam in December 1906 and again a year later. Without an FA degree, he left college and continued to pursue independent research in mathematics, living in extreme poverty and often on the brink of starvation."

## 间接引语（Hardy等人对拉马努金的描述）

### 15. "a mathematician of the highest quality, a man of altogether exceptional originality and power"
**来源：** G.H. Hardy, as quoted in Wikipedia
**原文：** Wikipedia - "Hardy concluded that the letters were 'certainly the most remarkable I have received' and that Ramanujan was 'a mathematician of the highest quality, a man of altogether exceptional originality and power'."

### 16. "defeated me completely; I had never seen anything in the least like them before"
**来源：** G.H. Hardy, as quoted in Kanigel
**原文：** Wikipedia - "After seeing Ramanujan's theorems on continued fractions on the last page of the manuscripts, Hardy said the theorems 'defeated me completely; I had never seen anything in the least like them before'."

### 17. "His natural simplicity and modesty have never been affected in the least by success"
**来源：** G.H. Hardy, letter, November 1918
**原文：** St Andrews - "Hardy wrote in a letter: 'I think we may now hope that he has turned the corner... His natural simplicity and modesty have never been affected in the least by success - indeed all that is wanted is to get him to realise that he really is a success.'"

### 18. "every time some matter... was mentioned, Ramanujan's response was an avalanche of original ideas"
**来源：** J.E. Littlewood, as quoted in E. Shils, *Reflections on tradition, centre and periphery and the universal validity of science* (Minerva 29, 1991)
**原文：** St Andrews - "Littlewood was asked to help teach Ramanujan rigorous mathematical methods. However, he said: '...that it was extremely difficult because every time some matter, which it was thought that Ramanujan needed to know, was mentioned, Ramanujan's response was an avalanche of original ideas which made it almost impossible for Littlewood to persist in his original intention.'"
