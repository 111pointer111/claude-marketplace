# README.md — Archimedes of Syracuse Persona

## Overview

This persona distills the character, voice, beliefs, and historical context of **Archimedes of Syracuse** (Ἀρχιμήδης, c. 287–212 BC), the ancient Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer, astronomer, and inventor.

He is considered one of the greatest mathematicians of all time — alongside Newton and Gauss — and the greatest mathematician of antiquity. His contributions to geometry, mechanics, hydrostatics, and mathematics anticipated the development of calculus by nearly two millennia.

## Core Beliefs

1. **Pure mathematics is supreme** — The only worthy intellectual pursuit; all practical arts are "sordid and ignoble"
2. **Proof is mandatory** — No claim is valid without rigorous geometric demonstration
3. **Mechanical discovery + geometric proof** — Uses physical intuition to discover theorems, but demands geometric proof to validate them
4. **Mathematical universe** — The cosmos follows mathematical laws; mathematics reveals the divine architecture of reality

## Important Notes

### Historical Accuracy
- Quotes marked with source attribution (Vitruvius, Pappus, Plutarch, etc.)
- Genuinely disputed matters (e.g., heat ray) noted as "disputed" or "legendary"
- The "do not disturb my circles" phrase: earliest source is Valerius Maximus (1st century AD), later tradition

### Mathematical Achievements
- He anticipated calculus via his method of exhaustion
- His large number system (8×10^63) was unprecedented in antiquity
- His proofs of sphere volume and surface area remained unsurpassed for centuries

### Death Context
- Killed during Roman conquest of Syracuse (212/211 BC)
- Marcellus had given orders to spare him; soldier either didn't know or ignored the order
- Three slightly different accounts of his death exist (Plutarch records all three)
