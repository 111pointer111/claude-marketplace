# CITATIONS.md — Archimedes of Syracuse

## Primary Sources

### 1. Vitruvius, *De Architectura* (c. 30 BC)
**Quote**: "Eureka, Eureka. I have found it."
**Context**: The golden crown problem — Archimedes discovers buoyancy principle while bathing and runs naked through Syracuse shouting his discovery.
**Original Latin**: "Eureka, Eureka. Καλὸν γεγονός" (the Greek phrase is preserved)
**Location**: Book IX, Chapter 215

### 2. Plutarch, *Life of Marcellus* (c. 100 AD)
**Quote 1**: "Archimedes possessed so high a spirit, so profound a soul, and such treasures of scientific knowledge, that though these inventions had now obtained him the renown of more than human sagacity, he yet would not deign to leave behind him any commentary or writing on such subjects; but, repudiating as sordid and ignoble the whole trade of engineering, and every sort of art that lends itself to mere use and profit, he placed his whole affection and ambition in those purer speculations where there can be no reference to the vulgar needs of life."
**Context**: Description of Archimedes' values and contempt for engineering
**Location**: Chapter XX (various translations)

**Quote 2**: "Oftimes Archimedes' servants got him against his will to the baths, to wash and anoint him, and yet being there, he would ever be drawing out of geometrical figures, even in the very embers of the chimney. And while they were anointing of him with oils and sweet savours, with his fingers he drew lines upon his naked body, so far was he taken from himself, and brought into ecstasy or trance, with the delight he had in the study of geometry."
**Context**: Archimedes' absorption in geometry overriding bodily needs
**Location**: Chapter XX

**Quote 3**: "These machines [Archimedes] had designed and contrived, not as matters of any importance, but as mere amusements in geometry; in compliance with King Hiero's desire and request, some little time before, that he should reduce to practice some part of his admirable speculation in science, and by accommodating the theoretic truth to sensation and ordinary use, bring it more within the appreciation of the people in general."
**Context**: Archimedes' view of his mechanical inventions as inferior to pure geometry
**Location**: Chapter XIV-XV

**Quote 4**: Three versions of Archimedes' death (see EVENTS.md for full text)
**Context**: Death by Roman soldier during siege of Syracuse
**Location**: Chapter XX

**Quote 5**: "It is not possible to find in all geometry more difficult and intricate questions, or more simple and lucid explanations."
**Context**: On the quality of Archimedes' mathematical works
**Location**: Chapter XX

### 3. Pappus of Alexandria, *Synagoge* (Collection), Book VIII (c. 290-350 AD)
**Quote**: "Give me a place to stand and I will move the earth."
**Context**: On the power of the lever — Archimedes' statement about mechanical advantage
**Original Greek**: "δός μοι ποῦ στῶ καὶ κινῶ τὴν γῆν"
**Location**: Book VIII

### 4. Valerius Maximus, *Factorum et Dictorum Memorabilium* (Memorable Doings and Sayings), 1st century AD
**Quote**: "...but protecting the dust with his hands, said 'I beg of you, do not disturb this.'"
**Context**: Archimedes' reported last words to his killer
**Note**: Similar to the tradition of "Noli turbare circulos meos" (Do not disturb my circles)

### 5. Archimedes, *The Method of Mechanical Theorems* (letter to Eratosthenes)
**Quote**: "Certain things first became clear to me by a mechanical method, although they had to be demonstrated by geometry afterwards because their investigation by the said method did not furnish an actual demonstration. But it is of course easier, when we have previously acquired by the method, some knowledge of the questions, to supply the proof than it is to find it without any previous knowledge."
**Context**: Archimedes' epistemological position on the relationship between mechanical discovery and geometric proof
**Note**: This work was lost until 1906 (Archimedes Palimpsest discovery)

### 6. Archimedes, *On Spirals*, Preface to Dositheus
**Quote**: "so that those who claim to discover everything, but produce no proofs of the same, may be confuted as having pretended to discover the impossible."
**Context**: Archimedes' response to mathematicians in Alexandria who claimed his theorems as their own; he sent them two false theorems to expose the behavior

### 7. Archimedes, *The Sand Reckoner*
**Quote**: "There are things which seem incredible to most men who have not studied mathematics."
**Context**: On the nature of mathematical truth transcending everyday intuition

**Quote**: (On his father) "My father was Phidias, an astronomer"
**Context**: Only biographical information Archimedes provides about his family

### 8. Livy, *Ab Urbe Condita* (From the Founding of the City), Book XXIV-XXV (c. 30-10 BC)
**Description**: Roman historical account of the siege of Syracuse and Archimedes' war machines
**Key content**: Description of Archimedes' engines causing devastation among Roman forces; account of his death
**Reliability**: Secondary source written ~180 years after the events

### 9. Polybius, *Histories* (c. 150 BC)
**Description**: Account of the siege of Syracuse from a historian's perspective
**Reliability**: Earlier than Plutarch or Livy; closer in time to the events

### 10. Cicero, *Against Verres* (70 BC)
**Quote**: "...and found it enclosed all around and covered with brambles and thickets; for I remembered certain doggerel lines inscribed, as I had heard, upon his tomb, which stated that a sphere along with a cylinder had been put on top of his grave."
**Context**: Cicero discovers and restores Archimedes' tomb in 75 BC, 140+ years after his death

### 11. St Andrews biography (J.J. O'Connor and E.F. Robertson)
**Source**: MacTutor History of Mathematics archive
**Quote compilation**: Includes all major quotations with source references
**URL**: https://mathshistory.st-andrews.ac.uk/Biographies/Archimedes/

## Modern Scholarly Sources

### T.L. Heath, *A History of Greek Mathematics* (Cambridge, 1931)
- Standard reference on Archimedes' mathematical contributions
- Quotes: "The treatises are, without exception, monuments of mathematical exposition..."
- Used for chronological ordering of works

### M. Chasles
- Quote: "...gave birth to the calculus of the infinite conceived and brought to perfection by Kepler, Cavalieri, Fermat, Leibniz and Newton."
- On Archimedes' integration methods

### M. Clagett, *Biography in Dictionary of Scientific Biography* (1970-1990)
- Quote: "Unlike the Elements of Euclid, the works of Archimedes were not widely known in antiquity."
- On the transmission of Archimedes' works through history

### Reviel Netz (Stanford University)
- Modern scholarship on Archimedes' mathematical methods
- Analysis of the Archimedes Palimpsest
- 2003 analysis of Stomachion puzzle (17,152 arrangements)

### J.L. Heiberg (1854-1928)
- Identified the Archimedes Palimpsest in 1906
- Professor of classical philology at University of Copenhagen

## Notes on Disputed Quotes

1. **"Do not disturb my circles"** (Noli turbare circulos meos):
   - Earliest known source: Valerius Maximus (1st century AD)
   - Later tradition through various late antique and medieval sources
   - Not in Plutarch's three versions of the death story
   - Considered "probable" but not absolutely verified

2. **"Give me a place to stand"**:
   - Source: Pappus (Collection, Book VIII)
   - Considered reliable — Pappus was a mathematician who would accurately preserve such statements

3. **"Eureka!"**:
   - Source: Vitruvius
   - Universally accepted as the famous account
