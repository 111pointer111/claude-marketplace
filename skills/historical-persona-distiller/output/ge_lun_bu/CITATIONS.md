## 第一次航行日记 (1492-93) — Wikiquote

### 1492-08-03
> "I should not proceed by land to the East, as is custom, but by a Westerly route, in which direction we have hitherto no certain evidence that any one has gone."

### 1492-10-08
> "Thanks be to God," says the Admiral; "the air is soft as in April in Seville, and it is a pleasure to be in it, so fragrant it is."

### 1492-10-12 (登陆 San Salvador)
> "...they could be much more easily converted to our holy faith by gentle means than by force, I presented them with some red caps, and strings of beads..."

### 1492-10-14
> "With fifty men I could subjugate them all and make them do everything that is required of them."

### 1492-11-27
> "When there are such lands there should be profitable things without number."

### 1492-11-27 (宗教限制)
> "...your Highnesses ought not to consent that any foreigner does business or sets foot here, except Christian Catholics..."

### 1492-12-16
> "...they have no arms, and are without warlike instincts... So that they are good to be ordered about, to work and sow..."

## 致 Santángel 信 (1493-02-15)

> "It is true that after they have been reassured and have lost this fear, they are so artless and so free with all they possess, that no one would believe it without having seen it."

> "I promise this, that if I am supported by our most invincible sovereigns with a little of their help, as much gold can be supplied as they will need..."

> "...as many slaves for the navy, as their Majesties will wish to demand." (1493-03-14)

## 致君主信 (1498)

> "Your Highnesses have an Other World here, by which our holy faith can be so greatly advanced and from which such great wealth can be drawn."

## 第四次航行信件 (1503)

> "The tempest was terrible and separated me from my [other] vessels that night, putting every one of them in desperate straits, with nothing to look forward to but death."

> "I came to serve you at the age of 28 and now I have not a hair on me that is not white, and my body is infirm and exhausted."

> "All that was left to me and my brothers has been taken away and sold, even to the cloak that I wore, without hearing or trial, to my great dishonor."

> "Weep for me, whoever has charity, truth and justice! I did not come on this voyage for gain, honor or wealth..."
