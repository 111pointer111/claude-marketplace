# John Locke — EVENTS Timeline

## 1632–1647: Early Childhood
- **1632** — Born August 29, in Wrington, Somerset, England, to a Puritan family of modest means
- Father serves as a legal clerk and cavalry captain on the Parliamentary side in the English Civil Wars
- Family environment shapes Locke's lifelong skepticism toward divine-right monarchy

## 1647–1652: Westminster School
- **1647** — Sent to Westminster School in London at age 14
- **1650** — Elected King's Scholar (a prestigious academic honor)
- Schooling is rigid and classical; Locke later criticizes its emphasis on corporal punishment

## 1652–1667: Oxford Years
- **1652** — Enters Christ Church, Oxford (late for typical入学 age of 20)
- Finds the Aristotelian curriculum dull; reads Bacon, Descartes, and new natural philosophers on his own
- **1656** — Receives Bachelor of Arts degree
- **1658** — Receives Master of Arts; elected Senior Student of Christ Church
- **1663** — Appointed Senior Censor at Christ Church; delivers Lectures on Natural Law
- Develops his two core philosophical commitments: natural law and empiricism
- **1666** — Meets Robert Boyle; begins medical research collaboration
- **1667** — Leaves Oxford to become personal physician and secretary to Anthony Ashley Cooper (Lord Shaftesbury)

## 1667–1683: The Shaftesbury Years
- **1668** — Becomes a member of the Royal Society
- Participates in a delicate surgical operation saving Shaftesbury's life
- Works as secretary for the Board of Trade and the Lords Proprietors of Carolina
- Begins work on *An Essay Concerning Human Understanding* (first drafts, 1671)
- **1675–1679** — Travels in France; studies French philosophy; stays in Montpellier
- **1683** — Following Shaftesbury's death and the exposure of the Rye House Plot, flees to the Netherlands

## 1683–1689: Exile in Holland
- **1683–1689** — Lives in exile in the Netherlands
- Completes *An Essay Concerning Human Understanding*
- Writes and publishes *Epistola de Tolerantia* (Latin, 1689)
- Maroons in a revolutionary era: the Glorious Revolution (1688) overthrows James II

## 1689–1704: Return and Late Life
- **1689** — Returns to England after the Glorious Revolution
- Publishes *An Essay Concerning Human Understanding*, *Two Treatises of Government*, and *A Letter Concerning Toleration* (all anonymously or pseudonymously)
- Moves to Oates, Essex, to live with Lady Damaris Masham (daughter of Cambridge Platonist Ralph Cudworth)
- **1690–1700** — Oversees multiple revised editions of the *Essay*
- **1693** — Publishes *Thoughts Concerning Education* (highly influential on educational theory)
- **1695** — Publishes *The Reasonableness of Christianity*
- **1696–1700** — Serves as the dominant figure on the revived Board of Trade
- Engages in famous philosophical controversy with Bishop Edward Stillingfleet
- **1704** — Health deteriorates; dies October 28 at Oates, with Damaris Masham reading him the Psalms

## Ideological Legacy

- **Political:** Direct influence on Jefferson's Declaration of Independence and Madison's constitutional design
- **Philosophical:** Founded British Empiricism (with Berkeley and Hume as successors)
- **Educational:** Locke's *Thoughts Concerning Education* shaped liberal educational theory in England and America
- **Religious:** His toleration writings remain foundational documents in the separation of church and state
