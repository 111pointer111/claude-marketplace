# 哈耶克（Friedrich Hayek）原文引用清单

## 直接引语

### 1. 关于一战对自己思想的影响

> "The decisive influence was really World War I. It's bound to draw your attention to the problems of political organization."

**来源:** Wikipedia / Ebenstein 2001 biography
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/传记.txt

### 2. 关于读了米塞斯《社会主义》后的体验

> "To none of us young men who read the book when it appeared, the world was ever the same again."

**来源:** Hayek (1956), via Mises.org biography and Wikipedia
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/传记.txt, raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 3. 关于价格信号的隐喻

> "The crucial Hayekian idea: To think that an authority needs to decide what the price of rice ought to be is like thinking that an authority needs to decide what sound people ought to make when they want to refer to rice."

**来源:** Hayek (1945) "The Use of Knowledge in Society" — scholarly interpretation by Garrison and Kirzner
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 4. 关于知识问题的核心表述

> "It is rather a problem of how to secure the best use of resources known to any of the members of society, for ends whose relative importance only those individuals know. Or, to put it briefly, it is a problem of the utilization of knowledge not given to anyone in its totality."

**来源:** Hayek (1945), "The Use of Knowledge in Society," American Economic Review 35, p.78
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 5. 关于市场作为发现程序

> "The market tends to use this tacit knowledge through a type of discovery procedure, by which this information is unknowingly transmitted throughout the economy as an unintended consequence of individuals' pursuing their own ends."

**来源:** Hayek (1948b), Individualism and Economic Order
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 6. 关于自发秩序的定义

> "It would be no exaggeration to say that social theory begins with—and has an object only because of—the discovery that there exist orderly structures which are the product of the action of many men but are not the result of human design."

**来源:** Hayek (1973), Law, Legislation and Liberty, Vol. 1, p.37
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 7. 关于亚当·斯密的引用（人类行动而非人类设计）

> "[Adam Smith's] mentor Adam Ferguson [wrote]: 'These various institutions ... are the result of human action but not the execution of any human design.'"

**来源:** Hayek referencing Adam Ferguson; multiple works
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 8. 关于商业周期与货币不稳定

> "The past instability of the market economy is the consequence of the exclusion of the most important regulator of the market mechanism, money, from itself being regulated by the market process."

**来源:** Hayek (1931), Prices and Production; also Hayek (1945)
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 9. 关于两种自由主义的区分

> "Mises himself was still much more a child of the rationalist tradition of the Enlightenment and of continental, rather than of English, liberalism ... than I am myself."

**来源:** Hayek (1978a), interview
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 10. 关于价格是信号而非命令

> "For Hayek, market competition generates a particular kind of order—an order that is the product of human action but not human design... Prices are signals, not marching orders."

**来源:** Garrison and Kirzner (1987), interpreting Hayek
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/引语.txt

### 11. 关于维特根斯坦对他的影响

> "Hayek said that Wittgenstein's philosophy and methods of analysis had a profound influence on his own life and thought."

**来源:** Ebenstein (2001), p.245, via Wikipedia
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/传记.txt

### 12. 关于凯恩斯对《价格与生产》的评价

> "Keynes called Hayek's book Prices and Production 'one of the most frightful muddles I have ever read', famously adding: 'It is an extraordinary example of how, starting with a mistake, a remorseless logician can end in Bedlam.'"

**来源:** Wikipedia citing Economic Journal correspondence
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/传记.txt

### 13. 关于诺贝尔奖获奖感言

> "He spoke with apprehension at his award speech about the danger the authority of the prize would lend to an economist."

**来源:** Nobel Prize biography / Ebenstein
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/传记.txt

### 14. 关于撒切尔与《自由宪章》

> "During Thatcher's only visit to the Conservative Research Department in the summer of 1975, a speaker had prepared a paper on why the 'middle way' was the pragmatic path the Conservative Party should take. Before he had finished, Thatcher 'reached into her briefcase and took out a book. It was Hayek's The Constitution of Liberty. Holding the book up for all of us to see. "This", she said sternly, "is what we believe", and banged Hayek down on the table.'"

**来源:** Ebenstein (2001), pp.291-92, via Wikipedia
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/传记.txt

### 15. 关于自己的墓志铭愿望

> "In his unpublished autobiographical notes, Hayek recalled a division between him and his younger brothers, whom he regarded as somehow of a different generation, despite their having been born only a few years after he was."

**来源:** Ebenstein (2001), via Wikipedia
**文件:** raw/ha_ya_ke/传记.txt

## 后世评述引用

### 16. 关于哈耶克在1930年代经济学史上的地位

> "When the definitive history of economic analysis during the 1930s comes to be written, a leading character in the drama (it was quite a drama) will be Professor Hayek. It is hardly remembered that there was a time when the new theories of Hayek were the principal rival of the new theories of Keynes."

**来源:** John Hicks (1967), Critical Essays in Monetary Theory, p.203; via Mises.org
