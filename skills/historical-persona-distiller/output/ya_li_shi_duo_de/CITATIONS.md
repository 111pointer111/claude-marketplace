# 亚里士多德 原文引用清单

## 来源说明

以下所有引用均来自已抓取的原始文本，并标注了出处。所有引用均可在原始语料中找到原文。

---

## 核心引语

### 1. 哲学始于惊异

> "Human beings began to do philosophy, even as they do now, because of wonder, at first because they wondered about the strange things right in front of them, and then later, advancing little by little, because they came to find greater things puzzling."

**出处**：《形而上学》982b12（Stanford Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Aristotle, Section 3

---

### 2. 真理高于友谊

> "In the interest of truth, one should perhaps think a man, especially if he is a philosopher, had better give up even theories that once were his own, and in fact must do so… it is our sacred duty to honor truth more highly than friends."

**出处**：《尼各马可伦理学》I.1096a.15（World History Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle, "Faith and Disagreements with Plato" section

---

### 3. 关于荣誉与善

> "Honor seems to depend on those who confer it rather than on him who receives it, whereas our guess is that the good is a man's own possession which cannot easily be taken away from him."

**出处**：《尼各马可伦理学》I.1095b.25（World History Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle, "Ethics" section

---

### 4. 美德的养成

> "A man becomes just by performing just acts and self-controlled by performing acts of self-control."

**出处**：《尼各马可伦理学》I.1105b.10（World History Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle, "On Acquiring Knowledge" section

---

### 5. 中道的定义

> "In regard to pleasures and pains, the mean is self-control and the excess is self-indulgence. In taking and giving money, the mean is generosity, the excess and deficiency are extravagance and stinginess."

**出处**：《尼各马可伦理学》I.1107b.5-10（World History Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle, "Virtue and the Golden Mean" section

---

### 6. 内省方法论

> "As in other cases, we must set out the appearances (phainomena) and run through all the puzzles regarding them. In this way we must prove the credible opinions (endoxa) about these sorts of experiences—ideally, all the credible opinions, but if not all, then most of them, those which are the most important."

**出处**：《尼各马可伦理学》1145b2–7（Stanford Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Aristotle, Section 3

---

### 7. 内省观点（endoxa定义）

> "Endoxa are those opinions accepted by everyone, or by the majority, or by the wise—and among the wise, by all or most of them, or by those who are the most notable and having the highest reputation."

**出处**：《正题篇》100b21–23（Stanford Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Aristotle, Section 3

---

### 8. 知识的目的

> "We think we understand a thing without qualification, and not in the sophistic, accidental way, whenever we think we know the cause in virtue of which something is—that it is the cause of that very thing—and also know that this cannot be otherwise."

**出处**：《后分析篇》71b9–16（Stanford Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Aristotle, Section 4.2

---

### 9. 模仿本能

> "Poetry in general seems to have sprung from two causes, each of them lying deep in our nature. First, the instinct of imitation is implanted in man from childhood, one difference between him and other animals being that he is the most imitative of living creatures, and through imitation learns his earliest lessons."

**出处**：《诗学》Part I（MIT Internet Classics Archive）

**原始位置**：MIT Internet Classics Archive, Poetics Part I

---

### 10. 悲剧的定义

> "Tragedy, then, is an imitation of an action that is serious, complete, and of a certain magnitude; in language embellished with each kind of artistic ornament, the several kinds being found in separate parts of the play; in the form of action, not of narrative; through pity and fear effecting the proper purgation of these emotions."

**出处**：《诗学》Part VI（MIT Internet Classics Archive）

**原始位置**：MIT Internet Classics Archive, Poetics Part VI

---

### 11. 第一推动者

> "Some force had to have set life in motion at some point in the past and this force — whatever it consisted of — he designated the Unmoved Mover or Prime Mover."

**出处**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle

**原始位置**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle, "Faith and Disagreements with Plato" section

---

### 12. 关于柏拉图理念论的批评

> "To claim that a horse is beautiful because of some unseen and unprovable realm of Perfect Beauty was untenable to Aristotle because any claim should require proof in order to be accepted."

**出处**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle

**原始位置**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle, "Faith and Disagreements with Plato" section

---

### 13. 逃离雅典

> "〔亚里士多德〕选择逃离雅典，'lest the Athenians sin twice against philosophy'（不愿让雅典人对哲学犯下第二次罪）"

**出处**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle

**原始位置**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle, "Later Life and Death" section

---

### 14. 康德对亚里士多德逻辑的评价

> "'That from the earliest times logic has traveled a secure course can be seen from the fact that since the time of Aristotle it has not had to go a single step backwards… What is further remarkable about logic is that until now it has also been unable to take a single step forward, and therefore seems to all appearance to be finished and complete.'"

**出处**：康德《纯粹理性批判》B vii（Stanford Encyclopedia引述）

**原始位置**：Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Aristotle, Section 4.1

---

### 15. 善的定义

> "Every art and every inquiry, and similarly every action and pursuit, is thought to aim at some good; and for this reason the good has rightly been declared to be that at which all things aim."

**出处**：《尼各马可伦理学》Book I（MIT Internet Classics Archive）

**原始位置**：MIT Internet Classics Archive, Nicomachean Ethics Book I, Section 1

---

### 16. 人的功能

> "If the function of man is an activity of soul which follows or implies a rational principle, and if we say 'so-and-so' and 'a good so-and-so' have a function which is the same in kind... if this is the case, human good turns out to be activity of soul in accordance with virtue."

**出处**：《尼各马可伦理学》Book I（MIT Internet Classics Archive）

**原始位置**：MIT Internet Classics Archive, Nicomachean Ethics Book I, Section 7

---

## 后世评价引语

### 17. 但丁的称呼

> "During the later Middle Ages (c. 1300-1500 CE), he was referred to as 'The Master', most notably in Dante's Inferno where the author did not need to even identify Aristotle by name for him to be recognized."

**出处**：World History Encyclopedia - Aristotle

---

### 18. 西塞罗的评价

> "If Plato's prose was silver, Aristotle's was a flowing river of gold."

**出处**：西塞罗（Stanford Encyclopedia引述）

---

### 19. 斯坦福百科对亚里士多德散文的评价

> "Aristotle's prose requires some acclimatization... Still, many of those who persist with Aristotle come to appreciate the unembellished directness of his style."

**出处**：Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Aristotle

---

### 20. 互联网哲学百科评价

> "Aristotle was known, in his time, as 'the man who knew everything' and later simply as 'The Philosopher', needing no further qualification as his fame was so widespread."

**出处**：Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy
