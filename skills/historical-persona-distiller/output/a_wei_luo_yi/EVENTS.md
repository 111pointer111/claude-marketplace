# EVENTS.md — Averroes (Ibn Rushd) Timeline

## Early Life (1126–1146)

### 1126 — Birth in Córdoba
- Born April 14, 1126, in Córdoba, Al-Andalus
- Son of Abū al-Qasim Aḥmad, chief qāḍī (judge) of Córdoba
- Grandfather Abū al-Walīd Muḥammad (same name) also served as chief qāḍī under Almoravids — a prestigious legal family

### 1130s–1146 — Almohad Revolution
- 1146: Almoravid dynasty overthrown by Almohad movement
- Family maintains position under new regime
- Ibn Rushd studies hadith, linguistics, jurisprudence (fiqh), and scholastic theology (kalām)
- Family's shift from Almoravid to Almohad patronage marks his early navigation of political-religious complexity

## Education Period (1146–1159)

### Medical Studies
- Studies medicine under Abu Jafar ibn Harun of Trujillo
- Develops systematic approach to natural philosophy through medical training
- Becomes proficient in observing natural phenomena and recording precise results

### Philosophical Influences
- Possibly studied under Ibn Bājja (Avempace), the Andalusian philosopher
- Avempace's influence visible in Ibn Rushd's works: emphasis on intellectual perfection, critique of Neoplatonic emanation
- Reads philosophical works of al-Fārābī and Ibn Sīnā (Avicenna) critically

## Rise at Almohad Court (1159–1169)

### 1159–1169 — Introduction to Caliph
- Through Ibn Tufayl (Abubacer), physician-philosopher at court, Ibn Rushd meets Caliph Abū Yaʿqūb Yūsuf
- Caliph asks him a difficult philosophical question about whether heavens are created
- Ibn Rushd initially frightened; caliph answers his own question, then sends him away with gifts
- Later re-engaged by caliph with commission to write correct commentaries on Aristotle

### 1160 — Qadi of Seville
- Appointed judge (qāḍī) of Seville
- Balances judicial duties with philosophical studies

### 1162 — Qadi of Córdoba
- Appointed chief qāḍī of Córdoba
- Serves in this capacity for approximately ten years

## Peak Productivity (1169–1195)

### 1169 — Commission for Aristotelian Commentaries
- Caliph Abū Yaʿqūb Yūsuf commissions correct interpretation of Aristotle's philosophy
- Ibn Rushd begins three-decade project of commentaries
- First major task: restore Aristotle to proper Aristotelian interpretation (removing Neoplatonic distortions)

### 1182 — Imperial Physician
- Succeeds Ibn Tufayl as personal physician to Caliph Abū Yaʿqūb Yūsuf after Ibn Tufayl's death
- Becomes chief physician of the Almohad court

### 1179–1180 — Major Theological Works
- **Fasl al-Maqāl** (Decisive Treatise): Philosophy is religious obligation
- **al-Kashf 'an manāhij al-adilla**: Exposes errors of Ashʿarite, Muʿtazilite, Sufi, and literalist theologians
- **Tahāfut al-Tahāfut** (Incoherence of the Incoherence): Defense of philosophy against al-Ghazali's attacks

### 1184 — Continued Service
- Caliph Abū Yaʿqūb Yūsuf dies; succeeded by Abū Yūsuf Yaʿqūb
- Ibn Rushd continues as chief physician to new caliph

### 1195 — Fall from Grace
- Religious conservative faction gains influence during jihad against Christian Spain
- Ibn Rushd formally rejected; exiled to Lucena (largely Jewish village southeast of Córdoba)
- His writings banned and many books burned
- Public attack on his orthodoxy — may have involved mob violence at jurists' and theologians' instigation

### 1197 — Recall
- Caliph Yaʿqūb recalls Ibn Rushd to Córdoba
- His disgrace was short but caused acute suffering
- Returns to Cordoba before death

## Death (1198)

### 1198 — Marrakech
- Dies December 10, 1198, in Marrakech
- Initially buried there; later transferred to family tomb in Córdoba
- With his death, philosophical activity in Islamic Spain substantially ends
- His works find new life in Christian and Jewish intellectual traditions
