# Nikola Tesla — Events Timeline (人物年表)

## Stage 1: Early Life and Education (1856-1884)

### 1856
- **July 10**: Born in Smiljan, Lika, Austrian Empire (now Croatia)
- Father: Milutin Tesla, Serbian Orthodox priest
- Mother: Djuka Mandic, inventor, could recite Serbian epics from memory
- One of five children; siblings included Daniel (older brother), Angelina, Milka, Marica

### 1863
- Brother Daniel dies in riding accident at age ~12; 7-year-old Nikola reports seeing visions — first signs of his lifelong mental sensitivities

### 1862
- Family moves to Gospic; Tesla enters local school

### 1873
- Attends Realschule Karlstadt, Germany

### 1875
- Enrolls at Polytechnic Institute in Graz, Austria; studies physics, mathematics, mechanics
- First sees Gramme dynamo — conceived way to use alternating current to advantage

### 1877-1879
- Attends University of Prague (auditing, self-study)
- Unable to find work in Maribor; returns to Prague; continues studies until age 24

### 1881
- Begins career as electrical engineer at telephone exchange in Budapest

### 1882
- **Feb**: Walking in Budapest park with friend, conceives rotating magnetic field principle; draws diagram in sand explaining induction motor
- Moves to Paris to work for Continental Edison Company

### 1883
- Assigned to Strasbourg; constructs prototype induction motor on his own time after hours
- Tries to interest European investors in his invention — no success

### 1884
- **June**: Arrives in New York with four cents, a few poems, and calculations for a flying machine
- Letter of introduction to Edison from Charles Batchelor: "I know two great men, one is you and the other is this young man."
- Hired by Edison to improve DC dynamos

---

## Stage 2: American Ascendancy and the War of Currents (1884-1895)

### 1884-1885
- Works at Edison's Manhattan lab; impresses Edison with diligence and ingenuity
- **Edison's broken promise**: Edison promises $50,000 for improved DC dynamo design; after months of work, Tesla delivers solution; Edison refuses to pay, saying "Tesla, you don't understand our American humor."
- Tesla quits after about one year of collaboration

### 1885
- Starts Tesla Electric Light Company; tasked with developing improved arc lighting
- Successfully creates improved arc lighting system
- Investors force him out of the company

### 1886
- Works as manual laborer, digging ditches for $2/day to survive

### 1887
- Finds new backers; Tesla Electric Company incorporated
- Operates own laboratory; experiments with X-rays, shadowgraphs, various types of lighting

### 1887-1888
- Granted more than 30 patents for his inventions
- Invited to address American Institute of Electrical Engineers on his AC work
- Lecture catches attention of George Westinghouse

### 1888
- Meets George Westinghouse in Pittsburgh
- Westinghouse purchases AC patents for $60,000 cash plus stock in Westinghouse Corporation
- Begins the "War of Currents" — Edison wages negative campaign against AC
- Edison attempts to demonstrate AC danger with public execution by electric chair

### 1889
- Invents "wireless electricity" method — high-frequency current transmitted through air
- Conducts research on wireless power transmission

### 1891
- **May**: Invented Tesla coil; patents the induction coil widely used in radio technology
- **July 31**: Becomes US citizen; tells friends he values this citizenship above many of his scientific inventions
- Lectures at American Institute of Electrical Engineers (pre-IEEE)
- Designs fluorescent light and early X-ray devices

### 1893
- **June 1**: Westinghouse wins contract to light World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago using AC system
- Tesla conducts demonstrations of AC wonders at the Exposition — "alternating current became standard power in the 20th Century. This accomplishment changed the world."
- Partnership with General Electric to install AC generators at Niagara Falls

### 1894
- Photographs Mark Twain using phosphorescent light — one of the first photographs lit by this method
- Begins writing autobiographical articles for Electrical Experimenter journal

### 1895
- **Fire destroys his New York laboratory** — years of research notes and X-ray equipment lost
- Despite setback, continues work; designs first AC hydroelectric power plant at Niagara Falls

---

## Stage 3: Colorado Springs and the Dream of World Wireless (1895-1905)

### 1896
- Niagara Falls power station operational — powers Buffalo, New York
- World's first large-scale hydroelectric power plant

### 1898
- Announces invention of teleautomatic (radio-controlled) boat
- Demonstrates the boat before crowds at Madison Square Garden in New York
- Proves claims for remote control technology
- **US Supreme Court later rules this patent invalid recognizing Tesla as radio inventor**

### 1899
- Moves to Colorado Springs to operate experimental high-frequency electricity station
- **Key discovery**: Terrestrial stationary waves — proves Earth can be used as conductor and made to resonate at certain electrical frequencies
- Lights 200 lamps wirelessly from 40 km (25 miles) distance
- Creates man-made lightning with flashes measuring 41 meters (135 feet)
- Believes he has received signals from another planet — scientific journals meet claim with derision

### 1900
- **January**: Leaves Colorado Springs
- Returns to New York; begins serious planning for Wardenclyffe project

### 1901-1902
- J.P. Morgan provides $150,000 funding for Wardenclyffe Tower on Long Island
- Tesla assigns 51% of telephony and telegraphy patent rights to Morgan
- Construction begins on 187-foot tower with 68-foot copper dome

### 1903
- Morgan withdraws support after dispute over tower's final use
- Financial panic, labor troubles compound difficulties

### 1905
- Wardenclyffe project effectively abandoned
- Staff laid off in 1906; site foreclosed in 1915; tower dismantled in 1917 for wartime security

### 1915
- False newspaper report announces Tesla and Edison will share Nobel Prize in Physics
- Neither ultimately receives prize (reasons unclear)
- Tesla reportedly refused to share prize with Edison if offered

---

## Stage 4: Decline and Eccentric Final Years (1905-1943)

### 1917
- **July**: Evicted from Waldorf-Astoria after 20 years for unpaid rent
- Receives Edison Medal from American Institute of Electrical Engineers — highest honor in field
- Tower demolished for wartime security reasons

### 1920s-1930s
- Successively evicted from St. Regis Hotel (1924) and Pennsylvania Hotel (1927)
- Lives in poverty in New York hotels; survives on modest savings and occasional consulting work
- Devotes much time to feeding and caring for wild pigeons in NYC parks
- Develops extreme eccentric behaviors and germ phobia

### 1926
- Publishes "When Woman is Boss" — articulates support for women's intellectual equality

### 1928
- Designs patent for "飞炉" (VTOL aircraft) — no prototype built due to lack of funding

### 1931
- **July**: Appears on cover of Time Magazine for 75th birthday
- Receives congratulatory letters from more than 70 Nobel laureates and pioneers in science, including Albert Einstein
- Given honorary doctorates by Columbia and Yale Universities

### 1934-1935
- Announces invention of "death ray" (peace ray) — claims it can destroy 10,000 airplanes at 400 km distance
- Draws attention from FBI; Soviet Union expresses interest during WWII preparations

### 1937
- Nominated for Nobel Prize in Physics
- **July 8**: Receives visit from King Peter II of Yugoslavia at Hotel New Yorker

### 1941
- Tesla Science Center formed (later to acquire Wardenclyffe site)

### 1943
- **January 7**: Dies of coronary thrombosis at Hotel New Yorker, Room 3327, age 86
- **January 12**: State funeral at St. John the Divine Cathedral, New York City; 2000+ attend
- Telegrams of condolence from Eleanor Roosevelt and Vice President Wallace
- Three Nobel Physics laureates speak — Robert Millikan, Compton, James Franck

### Post-Death
- US Supreme Court upholds Tesla's radio patents over Marconi's (June 1943)
- Papers, honors, letters, and lab notes impounded by Alien Property Custodian
- Later inherited by nephew Sava Kosanovic; housed at Nikola Tesla Museum in Belgrade
- 1956: Tesla's ashes returned to Belgrade in golden sphere (his favorite shape)
- 1960: Magnetic flux density unit "Tesla" adopted internationally
- 1975: Inducted into Inventor's Hall of Fame
- 2003: Tesla Motors founded (named in his honor) by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning
- 2018: Wardenclyffe Tower site added to National Register of Historic Places
