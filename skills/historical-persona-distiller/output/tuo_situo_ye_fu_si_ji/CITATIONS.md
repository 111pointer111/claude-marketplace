# 陀思妥耶夫斯基 — 原文引用清单

## 直接引语（按来源分类）

### Britannica — 大英百科全书

1. **关于心理深度**
"Dostoyevsky specialized in the analysis of pathological states of mind that lead to insanity, murder, and suicide and in the exploration of the emotions of humiliation, self-destruction, tyrannical domination, and murderous rage."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

2. **关于文学地位**
"Dostoyevsky is commonly regarded as one of the finest novelists who ever lived."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

3. **关于预言性**
"His works are often called prophetic because he so accurately predicted how Russia's revolutionaries would behave if they came to power."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

4. **关于文学影响**
"Literary modernism, existentialism, and various schools of psychology, theology, and literary criticism have been profoundly shaped by his ideas."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

5. **关于信仰立场**
"Dostoyevsky came to believe that western Europe was about to collapse, after which Russia and the Russian Orthodox church would create the kingdom of God on earth and so fulfill the promise of the Book of Revelation."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

6. **关于苦役背景**
"The major events of Dostoevsky's life—mock execution, imprisonment in Siberia, and epileptic seizures—were so well known that, even apart from his work, Dostoyevsky achieved great celebrity in his own time."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

7. **关于其早期背景**
"Unlike many other Russian writers of the first part of the 19th century, Dostoyevsky was not born into the landed gentry."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

8. **关于贫困与写作速度**
"Dostoyevsky was always in need of money and had to hurry his works into publication."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

9. **关于《罪与罚》拉斯柯尔尼科夫理论**
"Nothing could be further from Dostoyevsky's own morality, based on the infinite worth of each human soul, than this Napoleonic theory, which Dostoyevsky viewed as the real content of the intelligentsia's belief in its superior wisdom."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / Crime and Punishment" 条目

10. **关于《罪与罚》索尼娅**
"Having become a prostitute to support her family, she later persuades Raskolnikov to confess and then follows him to Siberia."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / Crime and Punishment" 条目

11. **关于《罪与罚》结尾**
"the prisoner Raskolnikov, who has confessed not out of remorse but out of emotional stress, at first continues to maintain his amoral theories but at last is brought to true repentance by a revelatory dream and by Sonya's goodness"
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / Crime and Punishment" 条目

12. **关于《白痴》的写作目的**
"Dostoyevsky's next major novel, Idiot (1868–69; The Idiot), represents his attempt to describe a perfectly good man in a way that is still psychologically convincing—seemingly an impossible artistic task. If he could succeed, Dostoyevsky believed, he would show that Christ-like goodness is indeed possible."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / The Idiot" 条目

13. **关于《群魔》的政治预言**
"It describes a cell of revolutionary conspirators... In lines that anticipate Soviet and Maoist cultural policy, Pyotr Stepanovich predicts that, when the revolution comes, 'Cicero will have his tongue cut out, Copernicus will have his eyes put out, Shakespeare will be stoned,' all in the name of 'equality.'"
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / The Possessed" 条目

14. **关于《群魔》的保守立场**
"The Possessed is a profoundly conservative and Christian work. In contrast to its savage portraits of intellectuals, the novel expresses great sympathy for workers and other ordinary people ill-served by the radicals who presume to speak in their name."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / The Possessed" 条目

15. **关于《卡拉马佐夫兄弟》伊万**
"Like Raskolnikov, Ivan argues that, if there is no God and no immortality, then 'all is permitted.'"
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / The Brothers Karamazov" 条目

16. **关于《卡拉马佐夫兄弟》阿廖沙**
"The youngest legitimate son, Alyosha, is another of Dostoyevsky's attempts to create a realistic Christ figure."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / The Brothers Karamazov" 条目

17. **关于大法官**
"Christ came to make people free, but people do not want to be free, no matter what they say. They want security and certainty rather than free choice, which leads them to error and guilt."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / The Brothers Karamazov" 条目

18. **关于平庸的魔鬼**
"The image of the 'petty demon' has had immense influence on 20th-century thought and literature."
— Britannica, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky / The Brothers Karamazov" 条目

### Biography.com

19. **基本事实**
"Born Nov. 11, 1821 in Moscow, Russia, Fyodor Dostoyevsky was educated at home until 1833."
— Biography.com, "Fyodor Dostoyevsky" 条目

20. **身份概述**
"Fyodor Dostoyevsky (born November 11 [October 30, Old Style], 1821, Moscow, Russia—died February 9 [January 28, Old Style], 1881, St. Petersburg) was a Russian novelist and short-story writer whose psychological penetration into the darkest recesses of the human heart, together with his unsurpassed moments of illumination, had an immense influence on 20th-century fiction."
— Biography.com / Britannica

### 北京大学期刊网

21. **关于基督与真理**
"陀思妥耶夫斯基本人曾明确地表示：如果有谁向我证明，基督脱离了真理，并且的确是真理也脱离了基督，那我宁愿与基督而不是与真理在一起。"
— 北京大学期刊网，《转喻的辩证法：陀思妥耶夫斯基的宗教修辞》
