## 一、生平与经历类引用

### 童年经历

**引用1：母亲精神崩溃**
> "When Jung was just 3 years old, his mother had a nervous breakdown and spent several months in hospital. In his memoirs of 1961, he wrote: 'From then on I always felt mistrustful when the word love was spoken. The feeling I associated with woman was for a long time that of innate unreliability.'"
— Psychology Today, "The Life of Carl Jung"
— 原文：`"从那时起，每当我听到'爱'这个词，我就感到不信任。我长期将'女人'与'与生俱来的不可靠感'联系起来。"`

**引用2：两个人格**
> "Jung was a solitary and introverted child. From childhood, he believed that, like his mother, he had two personalities—a modern Swiss citizen and a personality more suited to the 18th century."
— English Wikipedia, "Carl Jung"
— 原文：`"多年之后回想起来，他将自己分成了两个人格——一号和二号。一号性格是表现在每天的日常生活中；另一人格犹如大人一般，多疑、不轻易相信别人。"`

**引用3：12岁的昏厥事件**
> "At the age of 12, shortly before the end of his first year at the Humanistisches Gymnasium in Basel, Jung was pushed to the ground by another boy and hit his head, momentarily losing consciousness. A thought then came to him—'Now you won't have to go to school anymore.' For the next six months, he avoided going to school by fainting each time his parents tried to make him—an episode that gave him an early insight into hysteria."
— Psychology Today + English Wikipedia
— 原文：`"12岁那年他被推倒，此后数月内荣格经常陷入昏厥状态……之后荣格却通过自己意志力治愈自己的怪病。荣格将其称为经历一次'精神官能症'。"`

**引用4：木偶仪式**
> "He once carved a tiny mannequin into the end of a wooden ruler, which he kept together with a painted stone in a pencil case in the attic. He periodically returned to the mannequin, bringing to it scrolls inscribed in a secret language of his own making."
— Psychology Today
— 原文：荣格自述在阁楼中为木偶举行仪式，带来内心的平静与安全感

### 学术训练

**引用5：发现精神病学**
> "Here alone the two currents of my interest could flow together and in a united stream dig their own bed. Here was the empirical field common to biological and spiritual facts, which I had everywhere sought and nowhere found."
— Psychology Today, citing Jung's memoir
— 原文：`"只有在这里，我的两个兴趣才能汇合在一起，在这里找到了我一直到处寻找却找不到的生物学与精神事实的共同领域。"`

**引用6：加入Burghölzli**
> "In December 1900, he moved to Zürich and began as an intern at the Burghölzli psychiatric hospital under the psychiatrist Eugen Bleuler."
— English Wikipedia

### 与弗洛伊德的关系

**引用7：13小时长谈**
> "Jung met Freud for the first time in Vienna on 3 March 1907. Jung recalled the discussion between himself and Freud as interminable and unceasing for 13 hours."
— English Wikipedia
— 原文：`"1907年3月，荣格与弗洛伊德两人正式在维也纳会面，两人交谈了近13小时。"`

**引用8：弗洛伊德视荣格为接班人**
> "Freud proposed Jung, 'his adopted eldest son, his crown prince, and successor,' for the position of lifetime President of the newly formed International Psychoanalytical Association."
— English Wikipedia

**引用9：决裂原因**
> "Jung differed with Freud largely over the latter's insistence on the sexual bases of neurosis."
— Britannica

**引用10：1913年最终决裂**
> "In early January 1913, Freud wrote to Jung, proposing that 'we abandon our private relationship entirely. I will lose nothing, because I have long been joined to you only by the thin thread of the further development of past disappointments.' Jung agreed to comply with this arrangement."
— English Wikipedia

### 心理危机与红书

**引用11：与潜意识的正面交锋**
> "After the break between Jung and Freud in 1913, Jung went through a pivotal psychological transformation. He was on the verge of a psychosis. As the war burnt out, Jung gained first-hand experience of psychotic material in which he found 'a matrix of mythopoeic imagination which has vanished from our rational age.'"
— English Wikipedia + Psychology Today

**引用12：普菲斯特蒙**
> "Like Gilgamesh, Odysseus, and Herakles before him, he journeyed to an underworld where he conversed with Salome, a fair young woman, and with Philemon, an old man with a white beard and the wings of a kingfisher. In Philemon, Jung at long last found the father figure that both Freud and his own father had failed to be."
— Psychology Today

## 二、核心思想类引用

### 集体无意识

**引用13：集体无意识定义**
> "The collective unconscious is not so much a 'geographical location', but a deduction from the alleged ubiquity of archetypes over space and time."
— English Wikipedia

**引用14：集体无意识是普遍经验**
> "The contents of the collective unconscious are known as archetypes. They have a universal character."
— Britannica + Jung's own writing

**引用15：个人无意识与集体无意识**
> "For Jung, beneath the personal unconscious lay a deeper layer that contained the entire psychic heritage of humankind. This 'collective unconscious' had been pointed to by his childhood dreams and experiences and by the delusions and hallucinations of his patients, which contained symbols that recurred in myths and legends from all over the world."
— Psychology Today

### 原型

**引用16：原型定义**
> "The term 'archetype' thus applies only to those psychic contents which have not yet been submitted to conscious elaboration and are therefore an immediate datum of psychic experience."
— Jung, "The Archetypes of the Collective Unconscious"

**引用17：原型无处不在**
> "Archetypes are instinctive patterns, have a universal character, and are expressed in behaviour and images."
— Britannica

### 个性化

**引用18：个性化是人生主要任务**
> "Individuation—the lifelong psychological process of differentiation of the self out of each individual's conscious and unconscious elements. Jung considered it to be the main task of human development."
— English Wikipedia

### 内倾/外倾

**引用19：态度类型划分**
> "His first achievement was to differentiate two classes of people according to attitude types: extraverted (outward-looking) and introverted (inward-looking)."
— Britannica

**引用20：四种心理功能**
> "Later he differentiated four functions of the mind—thinking, feeling, sensation, and intuition—one or more of which predominate in any given person."
— Britannica

### 力比多

**引用21：力比多非纯粹性本能**
> "Jung replaced Freud's concept of libido with a much broader concept of undifferentiated psychic energy, which could crystallize into universal symbols."
— Psychology Today

### 共时性

**引用22：共时性定义**
> "Synchronicity is the experience of events appearing meaningfully connected despite having no causal relationship—a principle Jung developed throughout the 1920s and formalized in the 1950s."
— English Wikipedia

## 三、代表性引语（Quotes）

### 关于人性

**Q1：人生的目的**
> "As far as we can discern, the sole purpose of human existence is to kindle a light of meaning in the darkness of mere being."
— Jung, Memories, Dreams, Reflections (1962)
— Psychology Today引述

**Q2：关于阴影**
> "There is no doubt about the fact that man is, as a whole, less good than he imagines himself or wants to be. Everyone carries a shadow, and the less it is embodied in the individual's conscious life, the blacker and denser it is."
— Jung, Psychology and the Unconscious (1912)

**Q3：关于意识**
> "Until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life and you will call it fate."
— Jung

**Q4：关于完整**
> "One does not become enlightened by imagining figures of light, but by making the darkness conscious."
— Jung, The Philosophical Tree (1945)

**Q5：关于婚姻**
> "The pre-requisite of a good marriage, it seems to me, is the licence to be unfaithful."
— Jung, in a letter to Freud

**Q6：关于理性与情感**
> "Rationality is not all—there is no substitute for a sound emotional basis upon which understanding can rest."
— Jung, Psychological Types (1921)

**Q7：关于精神健全**
> "Show me a sane man and I will cure him for you."
— Jung

**Q8：关于个人成长**
> "The least of things with a meaning is worth more in life than the greatest of things without it."
— Jung

**Q9：关于童年的意义**
> "As a child I felt myself to be alone, and I am still, because I know things and must hint at things which others apparently know nothing of, and for the most part do not want to know."
— Jung, Memories, Dreams, Reflections (1962)

**Q10：关于生命的意义**
> "The secret is that only that which can destroy itself is truly alive."
— Jung
